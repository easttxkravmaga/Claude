# ETKM Content Templates

> Load etkm-funnel-master before using this skill.
> This skill provides structure only. Actual copy and scripts are built separately.

---

## How This Skill Works

Every piece of ETKM content belongs to one of three funnel stages.
Each stage has a different job, a different audience mindset, and a different success metric.

Before building any content, answer these three questions:
1. **Which stage is this content for?** (TOFU / MOFU / BOFU)
2. **Which persona is this for?** (See persona matrix in etkm-funnel-master)
3. **What is the ONE job this content must do?**

Then use the appropriate template below.

---

## TOFU Content Templates — Awareness Stage

**The job:** Stop the scroll. Deliver immediate value. Make them curious about ETKM.
**Do NOT:** Mention pricing, push for sign-ups, use heavy CTAs.
**DO:** Give something useful. Make them feel understood. Leave them wanting more.

---

### Template T1 — The Hook + Teach Format
*(Short video, Reel, TikTok, short-form social post)*

```
STRUCTURE:

[HOOK — 1 line]
The single most important line. Must stop the scroll in 2 seconds.
Formula options:
  - "Most people don't know this about [topic]..."
  - "[Number] things [persona] should know about [threat/situation]..."
  - "Here's why [common belief] is wrong..."
  - "If you ever find yourself in [situation], do this..."
  - A visual hook (demonstrate the concept before saying a word)

[TEACH — 3–5 points or 1 demonstration]
Deliver the actual value immediately.
Each point is specific, actionable, and understandable in seconds.
No lead-up. No teasing. Give them the goods.

[CLOSE — 1 line + soft signal]
One line that closes the loop on what they just learned.
Optional: A single curiosity-building question or invitation.
  - "There's a lot more where that came from."
  - "This is just the beginning of what we teach at ETKM."
  - "Want to know what to do next? [follow/link]"

METRICS TO WATCH: Views, completion rate, shares, saves, profile visits
```

### Template T2 — The Pattern Interrupt Post
*(Static social post, carousel, infographic)*

```
STRUCTURE:

[HEADLINE — top of image or first line of caption]
One bold, specific, unexpected statement about their world.
Formula: "[Common situation] + [unexpected reality]"
Example structure: "You walk to your car every night. Here's what most people miss."

[BODY — 3–5 short items]
Each item is a standalone insight.
Short. Bold header + 1–2 sentence explanation.
No jargon. No selling. No ETKM plugs.

[CAPTION CTA — optional, soft]
"Save this for later."
"Tag someone who needs to see this."
"Which of these surprised you?"

METRICS TO WATCH: Saves, shares, reach, profile clicks
```

### Template T3 — The Origin Story Teaser
*(Short video or written post — designed to prime for MOFU)*

```
STRUCTURE:

[RELATABLE OPENING]
Start with a moment the persona has lived.
"I remember the feeling of walking to my car at night and wondering..."
"There was a time when [persona situation] felt completely out of control..."

[THE TURN]
One sentence that changes the narrative.
"Then something shifted."
"And then I learned something that changed everything."

[THE PROMISE — not the resolution]
Leave the full story for MOFU. Create curiosity.
"That story is one I share with every new student. 
 Because it's not really about me — it's about you."

[SOFT SIGNAL]
"If you want to hear the rest, [follow / link in bio / comment X]"

METRICS TO WATCH: Comments, saves, link clicks, DMs
```

## MOFU Content Templates — Consideration Stage

**The job:** Build trust. Dismantle false beliefs. Capture contact info.
**Do NOT:** Hard sell. Lead with price. Be confrontational about objections.
**DO:** Tell the real story. Prove they can do this. Give them a low-risk next step.

---

### Template M1 — The Epiphany Bridge Story
*(Email, video, long-form social, blog post)*

```
STRUCTURE:

[THE OLD WORLD]
Describe the life before — using the prospect's language, not ETKM's.
Name the specific fear, frustration, or situation.
Make them feel: "That's exactly how I feel."

[THE FALSE BELIEF THEY HELD]
Name the thing they believed that kept them stuck.
"I thought [false belief]."
"I told myself [false belief]."
This is the bridge they'll cross.

[THE MOMENT OF CHANGE]
One specific, concrete turning point.
Not vague. Not motivational. Specific.
"Then [specific event/realization happened]."

[THE EPIPHANY]
The new understanding. The thing they learned that changed everything.
This is the bridge — and it must be something the PROSPECT can cross too.
"What I realized was..." 

[THE NEW WORLD]
What life looks like now. Transformation, not perfection.
Specific and believable. Not superhero — capable.

[THE BRIDGE TO THEM]
"Here's why I'm telling you this."
Connect the story to the prospect's situation.
Make it explicitly about them, not ETKM.

[SOFT CTA]
"If any of this sounds familiar, [specific low-risk next step]."
Primary: Free trial lesson / Free seminar registration / PDF download

METRICS TO WATCH: Email open rate, click-through, time on page, lead capture rate
```

### Template M2 — The False Belief Dismantler
*(Video, email, social post series, blog post)*

```
STRUCTURE:

[NAME THE BELIEF — NEUTRALLY]
"A lot of people we talk to believe [false belief]."
"If you've ever thought [false belief], you're not alone."
Do NOT shame the belief. Normalize it.

[VALIDATE THE LOGIC]
"That makes sense, because [reason the belief feels true]."
Empathy first. They had a reason to believe it.

[THE REFRAME]
"Here's what we've actually seen..."
Introduce the new frame through evidence or story — not lecture.
"One of our students, [first name], believed exactly this when she started. 
 Six weeks in, here's what changed..."

[THE PROOF]
A student story, a stat, a concrete example.
Keep it real and specific. Not polished marketing speak.

[THE INVITATION]
"The only way to know for certain if this is right for you is to try it.
 And the first one's on us." [CTA]

METRICS TO WATCH: Engagement, comments ("this is me"), lead capture from follow-up
```

### Template M3 — The Comparison Framework
*(Blog post, video, email)*

```
STRUCTURE:

[ACKNOWLEDGE THEY'RE RESEARCHING]
"If you're trying to decide between [Option A] and [Option B], 
 this is worth reading."
Frame as a trusted guide — not a sales pitch.

[GIVE OPTION A A FAIR HEARING]
Describe the alternative honestly and positively.
Name what it's genuinely great for.
"[Alternative] is excellent for [specific use case]."
Never dismiss or mock competitors.

[NAME THE FORK IN THE ROAD]
"The question is: what's your actual goal?"
Make it about the prospect's situation — not ETKM's superiority.

[POSITION ETKM FOR THE RIGHT PERSON]
"If your goal is [specific outcome], here's why Krav Maga is designed for exactly that..."
Speak to the person for whom ETKM is the right answer.
Don't claim ETKM is best for everyone.

[THE HONEST CLOSE]
"If [alternative] is a better fit for what you're looking for, we'll tell you that.
 But if [ETKM's specific strength] is what you need — we'd love to show you what we do."

[CTA]
Free trial lesson — "Come see for yourself before you decide."

METRICS TO WATCH: SEO traffic, time on page, lead capture, free trial signups
```

### Template M4 — The Student Transformation Story
*(Video testimonial, written case study, email story)*

```
STRUCTURE:

[INTRODUCE THE HERO — NOT ETKM]
Name, brief description, the situation they were in.
Relatable, specific, real. Not polished or idealized.
"[Name] is a [real, relatable descriptor]. 
 When she first came to us, she was dealing with [specific situation]."

[THE HESITATION]
Name the false belief or fear they had about starting.
"She almost didn't come. She thought [false belief]."

[THE FIRST WIN]
The moment something clicked. Specific, early, small.
Not the final transformation — the first spark.
"In her second class, something changed..."

[THE TRANSFORMATION (REALISTIC)]
Where they are now. Specific and believable.
Focus on identity shift, not just skill acquisition.
"Today, [Name] doesn't just know how to defend herself. 
 She carries herself differently."

[THE BRIDGE LINE]
One sentence that connects their story to the prospect.
"Her story isn't unusual. It's exactly what we see again and again."

[CTA]
"Your first class is free. Start where [Name] started."

METRICS TO WATCH: Video completion rate, shares, free trial bookings
```

## BOFU Content Templates — Decision Stage

**The job:** Convert. Make the offer irresistible. Remove the last objection.
**Do NOT:** Add new information. Introduce confusion. Bury the CTA.
**DO:** Stack the value. Show social proof. Make the next step obvious and easy.

---

### Template B1 — The Offer Stack Presentation
*(Landing page, sales conversation, email offer)*

```
STRUCTURE:

[THE HEADLINE]
One line. The transformation, not the features.
"Become the person who is prepared — starting this week."

[THE EMPATHY OPENER — 2 sentences max]
Name what they've been feeling. Name the turning point.
"You've been thinking about this. Here's how to stop thinking and start."

[THE VALUE STACK — Itemized]
Present each bundle item with its perceived value.
Format:
  ✓ [Item name] — [Specific description] ......................... Value: $[X]
  ✓ [Item name] — [Specific description] ......................... Value: $[X]
  [Repeat for each item]
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Total Perceived Value: $[X]
  Your Investment: $[price]

[THE REFRAME]
"For less than [relatable comparison — dinner out, gym membership, etc.],
 you get [total bundle]."

[SOCIAL PROOF BLOCK]
2–3 short testimonials. Real names. Specific outcomes.
Video preferred. Written acceptable.
Select testimonials that address the most common last-minute hesitation.

[THE FRICTION REMOVERS]
Transparent pricing. No hidden fees.
Risk reversal if applicable ("First class is free — no contract required to start").
Schedule visibility.

[THE CTA — Bold, Single, Specific]
"Schedule Your Free Trial Lesson"
Button / link / phone number — whatever the conversion path is.
One ask. Not two. Not three. One.

[URGENCY — Only If Authentic]
Only add scarcity or urgency if it is real.
"Classes fill up — [X] spots remain this month" (only if true).
Never manufacture fake urgency.

METRICS TO WATCH: Conversion rate, trial lesson bookings, cost per acquisition
```

### Template B2 — The Testimonial/Social Proof Framework
*(Video testimonial brief, written testimonial prompt, review request)*

```
WHAT TO CAPTURE IN A GREAT TESTIMONIAL:

1. Who they were before (specific situation, not generic)
2. What they were afraid of or hesitant about
3. The first moment something clicked
4. Where they are now (specific, believable, identity-focused)
5. Who they would recommend ETKM to

PROMPT FOR WRITTEN TESTIMONIALS:
"Tell us: What were you feeling before you started? What surprised you most?
 And what would you tell someone who's on the fence?"

VIDEO TESTIMONIAL BRIEF:
- Film in natural light, casual setting (not staged)
- 60–90 seconds max
- No script — natural conversation
- Focus on BEFORE → AFTER story arc
- Capture a moment of genuine emotion if possible

SELECT FOR: Relatability to target persona, specific detail, identity shift language
AVOID: Generic praise ("great instructor, great classes"), overpromise language
```

### Template B3 — Pre-Visit Nurture (Trial Lesson Booked — Not Yet Attended)
*(Email or text sequence between booking and first visit)*

```
STRUCTURE — 3 touch sequence:

TOUCH 1 — Immediately after booking:
  Subject: You're all set — here's what to expect
  - Confirm the booking details
  - Remove logistics anxiety (what to wear, where to park, what happens when you arrive)
  - One warm, welcoming sentence about what they'll experience
  - No selling

TOUCH 2 — 24 hours before:
  Subject: See you tomorrow — one thing to know
  - One sentence reminder
  - A student quote or 2-sentence story that addresses the most common pre-visit fear
  - Reconfirm logistics
  - No selling

TOUCH 3 — Day of (morning):
  Subject: Today's the day
  - Very short. 3–4 sentences max.
  - Warm, calm, encouraging
  - "We'll take good care of you."
  - No selling

METRICS TO WATCH: Show-up rate, no-show rate
```

## Content Calendar Framework

ETKM content should follow a consistent rhythm. This is the structural framework —
not the subject matter.

| Week | Content Emphasis | Primary Format | Stage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Hook content — persona pain point | Short video or post | TOFU |
| Week 2 | Story content — false belief dismantler | Video or written story | MOFU |
| Week 3 | Value content — teach something useful | Short video or carousel | TOFU |
| Week 4 | Proof content — student transformation | Testimonial video or story | MOFU |
| Monthly | Offer / CTA push | Email + social + landing page | BOFU |

> Monthly theme content (e.g., March = Distance Management) maps into this framework.
> Theme-specific content lives in the monthly content theme skill (to be built).
