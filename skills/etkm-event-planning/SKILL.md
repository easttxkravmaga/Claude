## Event Type Classification

Before generating any event package, identify the type. Each type has
its own funnel strategy, curriculum approach, and conversion goal.

### TYPE 1: PROSPECT SEMINARS

**Purpose:** TOFU/MOFU conversion — get prospects into a trial class
**Duration:** 2 hours, paid
**Stackability:** NOT stackable — 4-6 rotating themes per category prevent overlap
**Conversion goal:** Seminar attendee to trial class to membership

**Four categories with rotating themes:**

| Category | Themes | Lead Magnet | Target Segments |
|----------|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| Women's Self-Defense | 4-6 rotating themes | 10 Life-Saving Skills Every Woman Must Know | Women - Safety, Women - Empowerment, Women - Fitness |
| Youth Safety | 4-6 rotating themes (age-appropriate) | Bully Defense Packet for Parents | Parents, Teens, Pre-Teens |
| College Ready | 2-3 themes (dorm, social, travel) | Campus Safety Survival Guide | College Students, Parents of College Students |
| Intro to Krav Maga | 3-4 themes | Family Safety Checklist | General adults, fitness seekers, former martial artists |

**The ETKM Seminar Format (TYPE 1 only):**

1. Opening slideshow — what will be covered, what to expect
2. Real-life attack videos — 1-2 videos showing the actual scenarios they will learn to defend
3. Training block — 3-3-2 formula:
   - 3 strikes
   - 3 defenses
   - 2 drills
4. Teaching progression: Crawl-Walk-Run
   - Live demo by instructor
   - Break it down step by step
   - Dry run (students solo)
   - Partner work in stages
   - Build into drill when they get the hang of it
5. Close — woven conversion, not a hard pitch

**Woven Conversion Strategy:**
Conversion language is threaded throughout, not saved for the end.
At the beginning, let them know there will be a special offer at the end.
Peak their interest early so the close feels like a natural next step,
not a sales pitch delivered to tired people. They should be able to see
themselves doing this on the regular as they work through the seminar.

**Post-Seminar:** Hand off to standard nurture sequence (etkm-nurture-sequence).

### TYPE 2: STUDENT WORKSHOPS

**Purpose:** Retention and skill mastery for current members
**Duration:** 2 hours, paid
**Frequency:** Monthly
**Stackability:** Stackable — ongoing series, each is a standalone deep dive
**Conversion goal:** Retention, advanced skill development, possible private lesson upsells

**Format:**
- Pure hands-on. No slideshow, no theory lecture.
- Deep dive into a single skill or scenario
- May include technique PDF as reference material
- Small funnel — retention focused, not lead-gen

**Workshop Categories:**
- Ground Fighting
- Close Quarter Fighting (CQF)
- Focus Mitt / Thai Pad Work
- Law Enforcement / Security
- Armed Citizen Tactics (ACT)
- Handgun Defense and Retention
- Long-gun Defense and Retention
- Knife Defense and Offense
- Blunt Object Defense and Offense
- Women's Self Defense Training (Fight Back ETX — advanced)
- Youth Self Defense Training (advanced)

## Event Package Intake Questions

When generating a package, gather these inputs first:

### All Types

- Event type (1, 2, or 3)
- Event date and time
- Location (ETKM facility or off-site venue)
- Expected attendance
- Ticket price and pricing tiers

### TYPE 1 Additional

- Which category (Women's SD, Youth, College Ready, Intro)
- Specific theme or curriculum focus
- Lead magnet topic (or use default for category)
- Attack videos selected (or leave for Nathan)

### TYPE 2 Additional

- Skill or topic for deep dive
- Any prerequisite belt level
- Technique PDF needed (yes/no)

### TYPE 3 Additional

- Guest instructor name and credentials
- Topic and curriculum
- Duration (single session, half-day, full day, multi-day)
- Revenue structure (flat fee, split, hybrid)
- Co-marketing details from instructor
- Venue if off-site

## Complete Event Package — What Gets Generated

### 1. Master Timeline

Scaled by event type:
- TYPE 1: 8-12 weeks pre-event through 2 weeks post
- TYPE 2: 4-6 weeks pre-event through 1 week post
- TYPE 3: 12-16 weeks pre-event through 2 weeks post

Timeline phases: Foundation, Launch Marketing, Build Momentum,
Final Push, Logistics Lock, Day-Of Execution, Post-Event Follow-Up.

### 2. Email Sequence

| Type | Email Count | Arc |
|------|------------|-----|
| TYPE 1 | 9 emails | Announcement, Registration Open, 3-Week, 1-Week, Day-Before, Day-Of Welcome, Thank You + Lead Magnet, Survey, Nurture Handoff |
| TYPE 2 | 6 emails | Announcement, Registration Open, 1-Week, Day-Before, Thank You + PDF, Next Workshop Announcement |
| TYPE 3 | 10+ emails | Full campaign — see WF-003 CBLTAC as template |

All emails written in ETKM brand voice. PEACE slogans integrated as
attention anchors where appropriate. Merge tags in [square_bracket]
Pipedrive format.

### 3. Flyer Copy

Content only — design execution happens in Canva following etkm-brand-kit.
Includes: headlines, body copy, course descriptions, logistics block,
pricing, registration URL, lead magnet callout (TYPE 1).

### 4. Social Media Calendar

| Type | Post Count | Platforms |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| TYPE 1 | 12-16 posts | Facebook, Instagram |
| TYPE 2 | 6-8 posts | Facebook, Instagram |
| TYPE 3 | 20-25 posts | Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn |

Organized into phases matching the master timeline.
Canva image briefs provided for each post following etkm-brand-kit.

### 5. Lead Magnet PDF (TYPE 1 Only)

Auto-matched to category. Content generated in ETKM brand voice.
Delivered as bonus material — advertised as part of the seminar package.

### 6. Day-Of Run Sheet

Time-blocked schedule including:
- Setup and AV check
- TYPE 1: Slideshow, attack videos, 3-3-2 training block, close
- TYPE 2: Warm-up, deep dive training block, cool-down
- TYPE 3: Per instructor format with ETKM opening and closing
- Conversion moments flagged (TYPE 1 and 3)
- Emergency contacts and logistics

### 7. Post-Event Package

- Survey questions (customized by type)
- Metrics tracker (attendance, conversion, revenue)
- Testimonial capture prompts
- Contact coordination log (TYPE 3 — instructor follow-up)
- Revenue reconciliation template (TYPE 3)

## Automation Integration

### Pipedrive (via Manus)

- Event attendees are managed by Make.com, not a Pipedrive pipeline
- Post-event warm contacts are deposited into P1 Prospects with labels applied
- TYPE 1 attendees who convert move through P1 standard stages
- Label: event name applied to contact record

### Make.com (via Manus)

- Registration webhook triggers confirmation email
- Attendance confirmation triggers post-event sequence
- No-show triggers recovery email

### Email Delivery

- All emails delivered through Pipedrive
- Merge tags: [first_name], [event_name], [event_date], [registration_url]
- Arc classification not applicable to events — all attendees get same sequence
