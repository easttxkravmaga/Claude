# ETKM Brand Foundation

This is the single source of truth for ETKM brand identity, voice, and messaging
architecture. Every email, ad, social post, and conversation should align with what
is written here.

---

## Quick Reference — The Non-Negotiables

**Brand Promise:** Confidence, capability, and readiness — built through simple, proven self-defense.

**Controlling Idea:** ETKM exists to help everyday East Texans gain the confidence and
skills to protect themselves and their families from real-world threats, empowering them
to live with strength, freedom, and peace of mind.

**The Hero is always the student. ETKM is always the guide — never the hero.**

**Primary CTA:** "Schedule Your Free Trial Lesson." — Use this everywhere unless context requires a transitional CTA.

---

## Default Message Structure (Apply to All External Copy)

1. Acknowledge the hero's **internal problem** (empathy lead — not just the surface fear)
2. Position ETKM as **empathetic guide** with brief authority signal
3. Present the **3-Step Plan** (see reference file)
4. Add a **brief stake** (optional — never dwell)
5. Paint the **success transformation**
6. Close with a **bold, direct CTA**

---

## Voice Rules — Always / Never

**ETKM Always Sounds:**
- Confident and direct (short sentences, active voice)
- Warm and supportive (builds up, never shames)
- Grounded and practical (backs claims with specifics)
- Clear (if a prospect re-reads it, rewrite it)

**ETKM Never Sounds:**
- Aggressive or militaristic ("Destroy. Dominate. Eliminate.")
- Mystical or spiritual ("Unlock your inner warrior energy.")
- Fear-mongering (state danger calmly — always move toward solution)
- Overpromising ("Guaranteed to win any fight.")
- Cold or condescending

**Voice Quick Test:** Read it aloud. Does it sound like a capable, calm instructor
talking to a friend? Would a trauma survivor feel safe reading it?

---

## The 3-Step Plan (Use in Most External Messages)

**Step 1:** Attend a Free Trial Lesson
**Step 2:** Get Your Personalized Training Blueprint
**Step 3:** Become a Confident, Capable Protector

---

## Identity Transformation Language

Always show who the student is becoming, not just what they're learning.

| Before ETKM | After ETKM |
|---|---|
| "I hope I could protect myself." | "I know I can protect myself and the people I love." |
| Fear | Confidence |
| Doubt | Clarity |
| Hesitation | Decisiveness |
| Vulnerability | Capability |
| Hoping | Readiness |

---

## Prohibited Outputs — Never Generate These

- Aggressive, militaristic, or violent-sounding copy
- Overpromises ("guaranteed outcomes")
- Traditional martial arts jargon without explanation
- Fear-heavy content without immediately following with plan and hope
- Copy that positions ETKM as the hero
- Copy that would feel dismissive to a trauma survivor
- Specific year counts for Nathan's experience ("42 years", "43 years", etc.) — use evergreen phrasing only: "decades of experience", "over four decades", or "a lifetime of experience in self-protection"

## Final Check for Every Output

- Does this make the student the hero?
- Does it speak to their **internal problem** — not just the surface fear?
- Is ETKM the guide — empathetic and authoritative, never the center?
- Is the plan clear and simple?
- Is the CTA bold and direct?
- Does it leave the reader feeling **capable and motivated** — not scared or small?
- Does it connect back to the controlling idea?
