# ETKM CTA Architecture

**Version:** 1.1
**Established:** 2026-04-26
**Source Doctrine:** Donald Miller — *Building a StoryBrand*, *Marketing Made Simple*
**Governs:** Every CTA across every ETKM channel, format, and audience segment.

---

## SECTION 1: THE THREE NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES

These rules are not guidelines. They are structural requirements. A CTA that
violates any of them does not ship.

### Rule 1: Direct, Not Clever

**Miller's principle:** "If you confuse, you lose."

A strong CTA tells the reader exactly what physical action to take and exactly
what they will get. It does not require the reader to interpret, decode, or
imagine what happens next.

**The three most common CTA failures — all prohibited at ETKM:**

| Weak CTA | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| "Learn more" | Vague. The reader does not know what they will learn or what action learning requires. |
| "Get started" | Ambiguous. Started doing what? Where? How? |
| "If you're interested, give me a call" | Transfers the decision burden back to the prospect. Conditional language ("if") gives them permission to opt out. |

**The test:** Can a stranger read this CTA and know exactly what they will
physically do in the next five minutes? If not — rewrite.

**Correct examples:**
- "Attend a Free Trial Class"
- "Book Your First Class — Saturday at 10am"
- "Download the Free Guide"
- "DM Us READY for the Free Checklist"
- "Schedule Your Free Trial Lesson"

**Wrong examples — never use:**
- "Start your journey"
- "Take the next step"
- "See what we're about"
- "Join the movement"
- "Explore our programs"

### Rule 2: Two CTAs — Direct and Transitional

Every conversion-oriented piece of content must offer two levels of commitment.
Not one. Not three. Two.

**The Direct CTA — "Marry Me"**

This is the primary ask. The action that puts them in the room. Always gets
the dominant visual position — the button, the bold text, the largest type.

For ETKM, the direct CTA is always a variation of:
**"Attend a Free Trial Class"**

**The Transitional CTA — "Date Me"**

This is the lower-commitment alternative for prospects who are not ready to
show up in person. Always gets secondary visual position — smaller type,
below the direct CTA, no competing visual weight.

For ETKM, the transitional CTA is always a value exchange:
**"Download the free guide" / "DM us [KEYWORD] for the free checklist"**

**Structural rules:**
- The direct CTA always appears first and larger
- The transitional CTA always appears second and smaller
- They never compete visually — the hierarchy must be obvious at a glance
- Every carousel ends with both on the final slide
- Every landing page has both — direct in the hero section, transitional below
- Every email has one or the other — never both in a single email
  (emails are single-job; the sequence handles the escalation)

### Rule 3: The CTA Completes the Story Arc

A CTA only converts when the preceding content has moved the reader through
the hero's journey:

1. They recognize their problem (external → internal → philosophical)
2. They have been shown a guide (ETKM — empathetic + authoritative)
3. They have seen a plan (the 3-Step Plan)
4. They have glimpsed what failure looks like without acting
5. They have seen what success looks like with acting

The CTA is the moment the hero is asked to act. It is not a standalone
element bolted onto the end. It is the conclusion of the story.

**This means:** CTA language must reference the transformation — not just
the action.

| Functional CTA (acceptable) | StoryBrand CTA (correct) |
|---|---|
| "Attend a Free Trial Class" | "Take the first step toward never feeling helpless again — attend a free trial class in Tyler, TX" |
| "Download the Guide" | "Get the guide that 200+ East Texans used to take back their confidence" |
| "Book Your First Class" | "Your family's safety starts with one decision — book your first class" |

The transformation promise is embedded in the ask. The reader is not just
clicking a button — they are acting on the identity they just claimed.

## SECTION 2: THE FOUR-ELEMENT CTA STRUCTURE

Every CTA moment — whether it is a carousel slide, the bottom of an email,
the hero section of a landing page, or the final page of a PDF — is built
from these four elements in this order.

### Element 1: Transformation Statement

One line naming what the reader becomes by acting. Speaks to the internal
problem, not the external one.

**Rules:**
- Maximum two sentences
- Addresses the internal identity shift, not the skill gained
- Uses "you" or implied second person — never "our students" or "members"
- Contrasts the before and after in a single breath

**Examples:**
- "Stop feeling like a target. Start moving like someone who knows what to do."
- "You have been carrying this weight long enough. Put it down."
- "The version of you that hesitates — that version does not have to win."
- "You already decided your family matters. This is what that decision looks like."

### Element 2: Direct CTA

One clear, action-verb command. This is the button. The bold line. The thing
they physically do next.

**Rules:**
- Starts with an action verb (attend, book, schedule, register, sign up)
- Names the specific action and, when possible, the specific time/place
- Never phrased as a question
- Never conditional ("if you're ready," "when you're interested")
- Never passive ("classes are available")
- A command that respects the reader — not aggressive, not tentative

**Examples:**
- "Attend a Free Trial Class"
- "Book Your First Class — Saturday at 10am"
- "Schedule Your Free Trial Lesson"
- "Register for the Next Session"

### Element 3: Transitional CTA

One low-commitment secondary option for prospects who are not ready for
the direct CTA.

**Rules:**
- Smaller type, secondary visual position — never competes with direct CTA
- Always involves a value exchange (they give contact info, they get something useful)
- Uses a softer verb (download, DM, grab, get, send)
- Acknowledges their hesitation without judging it
- Leads into a nurture sequence — this is the entry point for Sequence A

**Examples:**
- "Not ready yet? Download the free Self-Defense Starter Guide."
- "DM us READY and we will send you the free checklist."
- "Grab the free guide — no commitment, just clarity."
- "Text CONFIDENT to [number] for the 5-minute safety audit."

### Element 4: Stakes Line (Optional but Powerful)

One short line naming what happens if they do not act. This is Miller's
"failure" element applied to the CTA moment.

**Rules:**
- One sentence maximum
- States a real consequence — honest, proportional, grounded in truth
- Real fear is a legitimate motivator. Naming the actual danger someone
  faces is not fear-mongering — it is honesty. The line is manufacturing
  fear that does not exist or inflating a threat beyond what is real.
- Real responsibility is a legitimate motivator. A parent who has not
  equipped their child, a provider who has not prepared — that weight
  is real and naming it is honest. The line is weaponizing guilt as a
  manipulation lever when no genuine responsibility exists.
- Real urgency is a legitimate motivator. Classes fill up. Schedules
  change. Post-incident motivation fades. Name real deadlines and real
  scarcity. The line is fabricating urgency that is not there ("only
  3 spots left" when there are 15).
- Always followed by the CTA, never left hanging
- Calibrate to segment sensitivity — the Survivor arc has different
  tolerances than the Protector arc. Check arc guidance in Section 3B.

**The principle:** Never manufacture what is not real. Fear, responsibility,
and urgency are tools — not weapons. Honest use builds trust. Manufactured
use destroys it.

**Examples:**
- "Because waiting does not make the threat go away."
- "Every week you wait is a week your family does not have this."
- "The risk is not that something happens. The risk is that something
  happens and you are not ready."
- "Hoping is not a plan."
- "That close call was a warning. The next one might not be."
- "Your kids are watching how you handle the things that scare you."

**Anti-patterns — never use:**
- "You could be attacked at any moment" (inflated — exaggerates frequency
  beyond reality to manufacture panic)
- "Don't let your family become a statistic" (dehumanizing — reduces
  people to numbers for emotional leverage)
- "Only 3 spots left — act now!" (fabricated scarcity — unless there are
  genuinely only 3 spots left, in which case state it plainly)
- "What kind of parent doesn't prepare their kids?" (weaponized guilt —
  attacks identity rather than acknowledging real responsibility)

### 3B: Arc-Specific CTAs

These CTAs are calibrated to the prospect's story arc — where they are
in their journey when they encounter the content. Arc classification
comes from the ETKM Audience Segments database and the Arc / Segment /
CRM Label Crosswalk.

#### Arc: The Protector

*"I need to protect the people I love."*
Dominant identity: Role-based (provider, parent, guardian).

| Element | Language |
|---|---|
| **Transformation** | "You already decided your family matters. This is what that decision looks like in action." |
| **Direct CTA** | "Attend a Free Trial Class" |
| **Transitional CTA** | "Download the Family Safety Starter Guide" |
| **Stakes** | "Every week you wait is a week your family does not have this." |

#### Arc: The Regainer

*"I used to be confident / capable. I want that back."*
Dominant identity: Historical (former athlete, former MA, post-injury, post-life-change).

| Element | Language |
|---|---|
| **Transformation** | "You have not lost it. You just have not trained it in a while." |
| **Direct CTA** | "Schedule Your Free Trial Lesson" |
| **Transitional CTA** | "DM us BACK for the free returning-to-training guide" |
| **Stakes** | "The gap between where you were and where you want to be is smaller than you think." |

#### Arc: The Awakened

*"Something happened — or almost happened — and I realized I was not ready."*
Dominant identity: Event-triggered. High urgency. Often recent.

| Element | Language |
|---|---|
| **Transformation** | "That feeling — the one that told you something needed to change — trust it." |
| **Direct CTA** | "Book Your Free Trial Class This Week" |
| **Transitional CTA** | "Download the free guide — What to Do After a Close Call" |
| **Stakes** | "The moment of clarity does not last forever. Act while it is loud." |

**Tone note:** The Awakened arc requires urgency without pressure. The
prospect is already motivated — the CTA's job is to remove friction,
not add motivation. Time-specific direct CTAs ("this week," "Saturday
at 10am") work well here because the prospect is looking for the next
available action.

#### Arc: The Quiet Builder

*"I want to get better. No drama. Just skill."*
Dominant identity: Membership (self-improver, disciplined, low-ego).

| Element | Language |
|---|---|
| **Transformation** | "No belts. No speeches. Just training that makes you harder to hurt." |
| **Direct CTA** | "Attend a Free Trial Class" |
| **Transitional CTA** | "Download the Training System Overview" |
| **Stakes** | "Skill does not build itself. But it does build quietly." |

#### Arc: The Survivor

*"I have been through something. I need to feel safe again."*
Dominant identity: Historical (trauma-rooted). Highest sensitivity.

| Element | Language |
|---|---|
| **Transformation** | "You do not have to be ready. You just have to be willing to start." |
| **Direct CTA** | "Attend a Free Trial Class — No Pressure, No Judgment" |
| **Transitional CTA** | "DM us SAFE for the free personal safety guide — no commitment, just information" |
| **Stakes** | *Omit.* The stakes line is contraindicated for this arc. The prospect already knows the stakes. Naming them risks re-traumatization. |

**Tone note:** This is the only arc where the stakes line is explicitly
prohibited. The transformation statement carries the full emotional
weight. The direct CTA includes reassurance language ("no pressure,
no judgment"). The transitional CTA includes explicit friction-removal
("no commitment, just information"). Every word must pass the
trauma-survivor voice test from etkm-brand-foundation.

#### Arc: The Professional

*"My job puts me at risk. I need skills that match my threat level."*
Dominant identity: Role-based (LE, security, high-risk occupation).

| Element | Language |
|---|---|
| **Transformation** | "You train for the job. This is training for the moments the job does not cover." |
| **Direct CTA** | "Register for the Next Session" |
| **Transitional CTA** | "Request the Course Overview for Your Organization" |
| **Stakes** | "The situation you trained for and the situation you get are rarely the same." |

## SECTION 4: QUALITY GATES

Before any CTA ships — in any format, for any audience — it must pass
every gate on this list.

### Structural Gates

- [ ] **Two CTAs present** — direct and transitional (exception: single-job
      emails use one or the other, never both)
- [ ] **Direct CTA appears first** and carries dominant visual weight
- [ ] **Transitional CTA appears second** with secondary visual treatment
- [ ] **Four-element structure used** — transformation statement, direct CTA,
      transitional CTA, stakes line (stakes optional for low-sensitivity
      segments, prohibited for Survivor arc)
- [ ] **Action verb leads the direct CTA** — not a question, not conditional,
      not passive

### Language Gates

- [ ] **No vague CTAs** — "learn more," "get started," "explore," "discover,"
      "take the next step," "join the movement," "see what we're about"
      are all prohibited
- [ ] **No conditional language** — "if you're ready," "when you're interested,"
      "should you decide to" are all prohibited
- [ ] **No manufactured fear, guilt, or urgency** — fear, responsibility,
      and urgency are legitimate when they reflect reality. They are
      prohibited when fabricated, inflated, or weaponized. The test:
      is this true? If yes, state it honestly. If no, cut it.
- [ ] **Transformation referenced** — the CTA moment includes identity-level
      language, not just a functional instruction
- [ ] **Prohibited vocabulary check** — per etkm-brand-foundation (no mastery,
      dominate, destroy, killer, beast, crush, elite, warrior, belts/belt-based)

### Story Arc Gates

- [ ] **Preceding content completed the arc** — the CTA does not appear
      until the reader has moved through problem → guide → plan → stakes → success
- [ ] **Arc-appropriate language used** — if the audience arc is identified,
      CTA language matches the arc entry in Section 3B
- [ ] **Tone matches segment** — especially for sensitive segments (IPV,
      trauma, teen/parent, LE). Cross-check with etkm-audience-intelligence
      and etkm-brand-foundation voice rules

### Brand Gates

- [ ] **Student is the hero** — the CTA asks the hero to act, not the guide
      to perform
- [ ] **ETKM is the guide** — any authority signals in the CTA moment
      position ETKM as empathetic + authoritative, never the center
- [ ] **Voice test passes** — read aloud; sounds like a capable, calm
      instructor talking to a friend
- [ ] **Trauma-survivor test passes** — would someone who has experienced
      violence feel safe, respected, and empowered reading this?

## SECTION 5: ANTI-PATTERNS

Common CTA mistakes that this skill exists to prevent.

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| CTA bolted on without story arc | Reader has not been moved — the ask feels random | Build the arc first. CTA is the conclusion, not an appendage. |
| Only a direct CTA, no transitional | Loses every prospect who is interested but not ready | Add the transitional. Always. |
| Transitional CTA competes visually with direct | Reader cannot tell which action is primary | Reduce transitional to smaller type, secondary position |
| Generic CTA across all segments | "Schedule a class" does not speak to a parent's fear, a survivor's hesitation, or a professional's standards | Pull from the Language Bank. Match the segment or arc. |
| Stakes line that manufactures what is not real | Fabricated fear, inflated threats, and fake urgency destroy trust. The reader knows when they are being manipulated. | Use real fear, real responsibility, real urgency. The test: is this true? If yes, state it. If no, cut it. |
| Clever CTA that requires interpretation | "Unlock your potential" — to do what? Where? When? | Replace with specific physical action + specific outcome |
| Multiple CTAs in a single email | Violates the one-message-one-job rule from marketing-engine | One CTA per email. The sequence handles escalation. |
| CTA without transformation language | Functional but forgettable. Does not connect to identity. | Add the transformation statement above the button. |

## SECTION 8: CTA DERIVATION ENGINE

The Language Bank in Section 3 provides pre-approved CTA language organized
by program and arc. But the right CTA for any specific piece of content is
not found by selecting from a menu — it is derived from what the content
itself is doing.

Every piece of ETKM content makes an implicit promise to the reader. It names
a specific problem, points toward a specific transformation, and leaves the
reader in a specific emotional and identity state. The CTA must be the natural
conclusion of that specific job. A CTA that introduces new framing, pivots to
a different problem, or asks for something the content did not earn will not
convert — regardless of how technically correct it is.

This engine is a four-step derivation protocol. Run it on any piece of content
before writing the CTA. The output tells you exactly which Language Bank entry
to pull — or whether the content is specific enough to warrant a custom CTA
built directly from the four-element structure.

---

### Step 1 — Read the Content for Four Signals

Before writing a single word of CTA copy, extract these four signals from the
content. They are always present. If any of them are missing, the content arc
is incomplete and the CTA will not convert — fix the content first.

**Signal A: The Problem Named**

What specific problem did this content address? Identify the layer:

| Layer | What It Sounds Like in the Content | What It Means for the CTA |
|---|---|---|
| External | "You could be followed to your car." "Crime is up in Tyler." | CTA can be functional — the reader is thinking about the situation, not themselves |
| Internal | "You don't want to feel helpless." "That fear is always there." | CTA must be identity-level — the transformation statement carries the weight |
| Philosophical | "Nobody should have to feel unsafe in their own community." "Your family deserves better." | CTA has a moral dimension — the stakes line can carry real weight |

Most ETKM content addresses all three layers. The dominant layer — the one
the content spent the most time on — determines the emotional register of
the CTA. Internal dominant = transformation statement leads. Philosophical
dominant = stakes line is strong. External dominant = direct CTA can be more
functional, less identity-heavy.

**Signal B: The Arc the Reader Is In**

Based on what resonated in the content, which arc does the reader most likely
occupy? Use the language the content used to identify it.

| If the content talked about... | The reader is probably in... |
|---|---|
| Protecting family, being there for people who depend on them | The Protector |
| Getting back to something they used to have, reclaiming capability | The Regainer |
| A specific incident, a close call, a moment of realization | The Awakened |
| Quiet self-improvement, no drama, just building real skill | The Quiet Builder |
| Healing, feeling safe again, moving past something that happened | The Survivor |
| Job-related risk, operational readiness, professional standards | The Professional |

If the content speaks to multiple arcs (a general awareness post), use the
ETKM General program entries from Section 3A and keep the CTA universal.

**Signal C: The Funnel Stage**

What stage is this content operating in? The funnel stage determines how
hard the direct CTA can push.

| Stage | Appropriate Direct CTA Pressure | Transitional CTA Role |
|---|---|---|
| TOFU (Awareness) | Low — the reader just learned about the problem. Ask is curiosity, not commitment. | Carries the primary conversion load at this stage |
| MOFU (Consideration) | Medium — the reader is weighing options. Ask is a low-risk next step. | Reinforces the direct CTA for hesitant readers |
| BOFU (Decision) | High — the reader is ready. Ask is the room, the booking, the commitment. | Secondary — the direct CTA is the job |

**Signal D: The Implicit Promise**

What transformation did the content point toward? This is the most important
signal. Stated in one sentence: "By the end of this content, the reader has
been shown that they can go from [current state] to [desired state]."

This sentence becomes the raw material for the transformation statement in
Element 1 of the CTA structure. Do not write the transformation statement
until Signal D is clearly identified — it must mirror the content's own
language and framing.

---

### Step 2 — Match Signals to the Language Bank

With the four signals identified, locate the correct Language Bank entry:

**If Signal B returned a clear arc AND Signal A is internal or philosophical dominant:**
→ Pull the arc-specific entry from Section 3B. This is the highest-precision match.

**If Signal B returned a clear arc but Signal A is external dominant:**
→ Pull the arc-specific entry from Section 3B but make the transformation
statement more functional and the stakes line lighter. The reader is thinking
about the situation, not their identity yet.

**If the content is program-specific (Fight Back ETX, ACT, EFC, Youth):**
→ Pull the program-specific entry from Section 3A that matches the program,
then calibrate to the arc from Signal B.

**If the content speaks to multiple arcs or is a general awareness post:**
→ Use the ETKM General entries from Section 3A.

**If no Language Bank entry fits the specific content precisely:**
→ Proceed to Step 3 and build a custom CTA. This is expected for highly
specific content (a post about a specific technique, a story about a specific
student, a campaign tied to a specific event).

---

### Step 3 — Build the CTA From the Content's Own Language

When a custom CTA is needed — or when refining a Language Bank entry to fit
specific content — build each element using the content's own language as
the raw material.

**Transformation Statement — built from Signal D**

The implicit promise the content made, made explicit. The transformation
statement must echo the specific language the content used. It does not
introduce new framing.

| Content Used This Language | Transformation Statement Should Echo It |
|---|---|
| "Freezing when it matters" | "Stop freezing. Start deciding." |
| "Feeling like a target in your own neighborhood" | "Stop feeling like a target. Start moving like someone who knows what to do." |
| "Your kids watching how you handle fear" | "You already decided your family matters. This is what that decision looks like." |
| "Wishing you had started sooner" | "The version of you that wishes they had started — they started today." |

Never introduce a transformation the content did not earn. If the content
talked about freezing, the transformation statement is about freezing. If the
content talked about a parent's responsibility, the transformation statement
is about that. The reader should read the transformation statement and think:
*that is exactly what I was just thinking about.*

**Direct CTA — built from Signal C (funnel stage)**

| Funnel Stage | Direct CTA Calibration |
|---|---|
| TOFU | Softer ask — "Download the free guide" / "DM us [KEYWORD]" can serve as the direct CTA at this stage |
| MOFU | Standard ask — "Schedule Your Free Trial Lesson" / "Attend a Free Trial Class" |
| BOFU | Specific ask — name the time, the date, the next available session. Remove all friction. |

**Transitional CTA — built from Signal B (arc)**

The transitional CTA should offer something that serves the specific arc the
reader is in — not a generic download.

| Arc | Transitional CTA Direction |
|---|---|
| Protector | Family safety guide, "what to teach your kids" resource |
| Regainer | Returning-to-training guide, "where to start" resource |
| Awakened | Post-incident resource, "what to do next" guide |
| Quiet Builder | Training system overview, technique breakdown |
| Survivor | Personal safety guide — always "no commitment, just information" framing |
| Professional | Course overview, department brief, program summary |

**Stakes Line — built from Signal A (problem layer) and Signal B (arc)**

The stakes line names what happens without acting. It must be true — not
manufactured. The content itself already told you what the real stakes are.
Use that. Do not invent stakes the content did not name.

| Content Named This as the Real Risk | Stakes Line |
|---|---|
| Freezing in a real situation | "Freezing is a skill too. It just works against you." |
| A close call that could have gone differently | "That close call was a warning. The next one might not be." |
| A family member's safety | "Every week you wait is a week your family does not have this." |
| Professional readiness gap | "The call you trained for and the call you get are rarely the same." |
| General unpreparedness | "Hoping is not a plan." |

**Survivor arc exception:** Stakes line is always omitted. See Section 3B.

---

### Step 4 — Verify the Arc Is Complete

The CTA only converts when the preceding content did its job. Before placing
the CTA, run this check against the content:

| Arc Stage | Check |
|---|---|
| Problem named | Did the content address the internal problem — not just the external situation? |
| Guide present | Is ETKM positioned as empathetic and authoritative — not as the hero? |
| Plan implied | Did the content make the path forward feel simple and achievable? |
| Stakes acknowledged | Did the content briefly name what happens without acting — without dwelling? |
| Success visible | Did the content show who the reader becomes by acting? |

**If any stage is missing:** The CTA will not convert. The missing stage is the
real problem — not the CTA language. Fix the content arc before writing the CTA.

**If all stages are present:** The CTA is the final beat. The reader has been
moved. The ask is earned. Write it, place it, ship it.

### Derivation Engine — Quick Reference

```
STEP 1: Extract four signals from the content
  A — Problem layer (external / internal / philosophical)
  B — Reader arc (Protector / Regainer / Awakened / Quiet Builder / Survivor / Professional)
  C — Funnel stage (TOFU / MOFU / BOFU)
  D — Implicit promise (from [current state] to [desired state])

STEP 2: Match to Language Bank
  Clear arc + internal/philosophical dominant → Section 3B arc entry
  Program-specific content → Section 3A program entry, calibrated to arc
  Multi-arc or general → Section 3A ETKM General
  No entry fits → proceed to Step 3

STEP 3: Build custom CTA from content's own language
  Transformation statement ← Signal D (echo the content's language)
  Direct CTA ← Signal C (calibrated to funnel stage)
  Transitional CTA ← Signal B (arc-matched resource)
  Stakes line ← Signal A + B (true, proportional, from the content itself)

STEP 4: Verify arc completion before placing the CTA
  Problem named → Guide present → Plan implied → Stakes acknowledged → Success visible
  Missing stage = content problem, not CTA problem. Fix the content first.
```
