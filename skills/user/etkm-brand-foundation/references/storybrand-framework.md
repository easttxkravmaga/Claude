# StoryBrand Framework

## Framework Overview

ETKM uses the StoryBrand 7-part narrative structure for all external-facing campaigns, landing pages, email sequences, and major ad copy. Non-negotiable: the student is always the hero. ETKM is always the guide.

---

## The 7-Part ETKM StoryBrand Script

### 1. The Character (Hero)

**Who they are:** An everyday East Texan who wants to feel safe and capable.

**What they want:** To know they can protect themselves and the people they love if something bad happens.

**Internal desire (deeper than the surface want):** To stop feeling vulnerable, afraid, or caught off-guard. To feel like a capable adult who has handled an important responsibility.

> "I want to know I could actually do something if I had to."

---

### 2. The Problem

**External problem:** Real threats exist — violent crime, carjackings, domestic incidents, workplace violence.

**Internal problem:** The fear and self-doubt that comes with feeling unprepared. The quiet shame of knowing you would not know what to do. The helplessness of worrying about someone you love.

**Philosophical problem:** Every person deserves to feel safe. No one should have to live in fear or feel powerless to protect their family.

> Lead with the internal problem. The external problem is just the context. The internal problem is why they are searching.

---

### 3. The Guide (ETKM)

**Empathy:** We understand what it feels like to be uncertain about whether you could protect yourself. We work with people who have never trained before every single day.

**Authority:** Krav Maga is the system used by Israel's military and law enforcement — adapted for everyday civilians. Our instructors are certified, experienced, and trained to teach real-world application, not sport competition.

> Show empathy first. Authority second. Never lead with credentials — lead with understanding.

---

### 4. The Plan

Keep the plan simple. Three steps. No ambiguity.

**Step 1:** Attend a Free Trial Lesson
**Step 2:** Get Your Personalized Training Blueprint
**Step 3:** Become a Confident, Capable Protector

The plan removes the risk of taking action. The Free Trial Lesson eliminates financial commitment as a barrier. The Blueprint makes the path forward feel personalized and clear.

---

### 5. The Call to Action

**Primary CTA:** "Schedule Your Free Trial Lesson."

Use this everywhere unless a transitional CTA is required (lead magnet download, video watch, etc.). All transitional CTAs should lead back to this primary CTA at step 2.

**Direct CTA (buttons, headlines):** Schedule Your Free Trial Lesson
**Conversational CTA (email, body copy):** "The first step is simple — schedule your free trial lesson and come try a class."

---

### 6. Success (The Transformation)

Paint the picture of who the student becomes — not just what they learn.

The outcome is not: "You will know 12 Krav Maga techniques."
The outcome is: "You will walk through your day with your head up, your awareness on, and the quiet confidence of someone who knows they could handle whatever comes."

| Before ETKM | After ETKM |
|---|---|
| "I hope nothing happens" | "I know I am ready if something does" |
| Anxiety when walking to the car | Calm awareness |
| Doubt about protecting family | Confidence |
| Feeling like a target | Feeling capable |

---

### 7. Failure (The Stakes)

Use stakes sparingly. State them once, calmly, then move toward the solution. Never dwell.

How to use: A single sentence that acknowledges the cost of staying unprepared — then immediately pivot to the plan.

> "The only thing worse than feeling unprepared today is feeling unprepared when it matters. That is what the free trial lesson is for."

Never use multiple paragraphs about crime statistics, worst-case scenarios, or graphic threat descriptions. State the stakes once, then offer the path forward.

---

## Format Calibration by Channel

**Landing page:** Hit all 7 parts in order, with CTA buttons at the Stakes section and the end.

**Short email:** Open with the internal problem (2 sentences), position ETKM as guide (1 sentence), give the plan (3 bullets), close with CTA.

**Ad (30 seconds or 3 sentences):** Problem → Guide → CTA. Skip stakes in ultra-short formats.

**Video scripts:** Full 7-part arc. Pacing: problem (30%), guide (15%), plan (25%), success transformation (20%), CTA (10%).
