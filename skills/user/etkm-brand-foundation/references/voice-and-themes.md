# Voice and Themes

## Voice Overview

The ETKM voice is the voice of a calm, competent instructor who speaks to students like they are already capable — they just do not know it yet. Think of the best teacher you have ever had: direct, warm, never condescending, never theatrical.

---

## Tone Registers by Context

Different ETKM content operates at different emotional registers. Match the register to the channel.

### Ads (Facebook, Instagram, Google)

**Register:** Urgent-but-calm. Empathetic opener, clear plan, bold CTA.
**Sentence length:** Short. 5–10 words per sentence.
**Avoid:** Hype, all-caps, aggressive imagery, militaristic language.
**Model tone:** "You do not have to wonder if you could protect your family. Find out — free."

### Email (Nurture Sequences, Campaigns)

**Register:** Conversational and direct. One idea per email. Build trust over time.
**Sentence length:** Medium. Write like you are talking to a neighbor you respect.
**Avoid:** Overcrowding with too many points. Sales pressure in early nurture emails.
**Model tone:** "Most people never train because they are waiting to feel ready. Here is the thing: the training is what makes you ready."

### Landing Pages

**Register:** Clear and guiding. The reader should always know exactly where they are and what to do next.
**Sentence length:** Mixed. Short for headlines. Medium for body copy.
**Avoid:** Jargon, passive voice, vague benefit language ("unlock your potential").
**Model tone:** Structured per the StoryBrand framework. Headlines state the transformation. Body copy earns it.

### Social Posts (Organic)

**Register:** Community-forward. Celebrate student wins, share training insight, provide value.
**Sentence length:** Varies. Hooks should be punchy (1–2 sentences). Value content can run longer.
**Avoid:** Fake urgency, hollow motivational quotes, content that could have come from any gym.
**Model tone:** "What most people call a dangerous situation is really just a moment where awareness, distance, and a clear head can change everything. That is what we train."

### In-Person / Video Scripts

**Register:** Warm and authoritative. Nathan's natural voice — measured, knowledgeable, never showing off.
**Avoid:** Scripted-sounding language. Write for how Nathan actually speaks.

## Recurring Themes

These themes run through all ETKM content. Use them consistently — they build recognition and reinforce the brand's controlling idea.

### Theme 1 — Confidence Through Preparation

The transformation ETKM offers is not a skill set. It is a state of mind. Students do not just learn techniques — they stop living in low-grade fear. Use this theme in success stories, CTAs, and benefit language.

### Theme 2 — Practical Over Impressive

ETKM is not martial arts performance. Every technique exists because it works under stress, against a larger opponent, in the real world. This theme differentiates ETKM from sport gyms and traditional martial arts schools.

### Theme 3 — Everyday People Doing Something Real

ETKM students are not action heroes. They are parents, teachers, nurses, retirees, college students. Content should reflect this. Testimonials, photos, and scenarios should feature relatable people — not intimidating athletes.

### Theme 4 — Family Protection

Many students come to ETKM specifically because they want to protect their families. This theme is powerful but must be handled carefully — never exploit fear, always move toward empowerment. "Protect your family" works when paired with the plan and the transformation.

### Theme 5 — East Texas Community

ETKM is local. The students are neighbors. This is not a franchise or an online program. Lean into the local identity — it is a differentiator in a world of generic self-defense content.

## Word Bank — Use These

**High-resonance words for ETKM content:**
confident, capable, ready, prepared, aware, calm, clear, decisive, practical, real-world, everyday, protect, family, community, free trial, step, plan, simple, proven, effective

**Transition phrases that work:**
"The first step is simple..." / "Here is what that looks like..." / "Most people do not realize..." / "That is exactly why..." / "You do not have to..."

---

## Word Bank — Avoid These

**Overused self-defense clichés:**
dominate, destroy, warrior, beast mode, lethal, weapon, elite, tactical, savage, brutal, badass

**Fear-heavy openers:**
"Every 90 seconds someone is attacked..." / "You are more vulnerable than you think..." / "When danger strikes..."

**Vague benefit language:**
"unlock your potential" / "transform your life" / "take your training to the next level"

## The Voice Quick Test

Read any piece of ETKM copy aloud. Ask:

1. Does it sound like a capable, calm instructor talking to a friend?
2. Would a trauma survivor feel safe — not scared or judged — reading this?
3. Is the student the hero of this story, or did ETKM accidentally become the center?
4. Is every sentence earning its place, or is there padding?
5. Does the CTA feel like a natural next step, not a sales push?

If the answer to all five is yes: ship it.
