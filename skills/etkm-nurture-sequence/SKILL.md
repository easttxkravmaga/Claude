# ETKM Nurture Sequence

> Load etkm-funnel-master before using this skill.
> Load etkm-brand-foundation when writing actual copy for any sequence.
> This skill is the engine that moves leads from opt-in to trial lesson booking.

---

## The Nurture Philosophy

Most businesses treat nurture sequences as a countdown to the sale.
ETKM treats them as a **relationship being built in real time.**

The prospect gave ETKM their contact information. That's a small act of trust.
The nurture sequence's only job is to honor that trust by consistently delivering
value until the prospect is ready to take the next step — on their timeline, not ETKM's.

**The three rules that govern every sequence:**

1. **Every touch must earn the next one.** If a message doesn't deliver value, it burns
   trust instead of building it. When in doubt, give more — ask less.

2. **The ask always comes after the value — never before it.** The CTA is the last thing,
   not the first. Prospects feel the difference immediately.

3. **One message. One job.** Never put two ideas in one email. Never include two CTAs.
   A prospect who gets to the end of a message and isn't sure what to do next will do nothing.

---

## Sequence Types — Overview

ETKM runs four distinct nurture sequences based on entry point:

| Sequence | Trigger | Primary Goal | Avg. Length |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A — PDF Download** | Prospect downloads a PDF lead magnet | Move from curious → committed enough to book trial | 7 touches over 14 days |
| **B — Seminar Registration** | Prospect registers for free seminar | Confirm attendance, build anticipation, follow up post-event | 5 touches over 7 days |
| **C — Pre-Visit** | Trial lesson booked, not yet attended | Maximize show-up rate, reduce cold feet | 3 touches over 24–48 hrs |
| **D — Re-Engagement** | Lead has gone cold (no open in 30+ days) | Reactivate or cleanly close the loop | 3 touches over 7 days |

## Sequence A — PDF Download Nurture

**Entry trigger:** Prospect submits name + email to download a PDF lead magnet.
**Goal:** Build trust through consistent value delivery. Move prospect to trial lesson booking.
**Channel:** Email primary. Optional SMS for Touch 1 and Touch 4 only.

---

### Touch A1 — Immediate (0–5 minutes after opt-in)

**Job:** Deliver the PDF. Warm welcome. Set the relationship tone.
**Channel:** Email (+ optional SMS confirmation)
**Length:** Short. 3–5 sentences of copy beyond the delivery line.

```
SUBJECT LINE OPTIONS (choose one per campaign):
— "Your guide is here, [First Name]"
— "Here's what you asked for, [First Name]"
— "[Guide Name] — just for you"

STRUCTURE:

[DELIVERY LINE]
"[First Name], your copy of [Guide Name] is attached / linked below."
One line. Get it to them immediately. No preamble.

[WARM OPENER — 2 sentences]
Name what they're about to learn and why it matters.
Speak to the internal motivation — not the external surface need.
"Whether you downloaded this because [specific persona situation] — this is 
 the right starting point."

[PERMISSION / WHAT'S NEXT — 1–2 sentences]
Let them know what to expect from ETKM going forward.
Keep it light. No pressure. No sales language.
"Over the next few days, we'll send you a few more things we think you'll find useful.
 No fluff — just what's actually worth knowing."

[SIGN-OFF]
Warm, personal, direct.
"Stay safe out there.
 — [Instructor Name], East Texas Krav Maga"

NO CTA in this email. Just deliver and welcome.
```

---

### Touch A2 — Day 2

**Job:** Add a second layer of value. Reinforce that ETKM knows their world.
**Channel:** Email
**Length:** Medium. 100–150 words of copy.

```
SUBJECT LINE OPTIONS:
— "One thing most people get wrong about [topic]"
— "The part the guide didn't cover"
— "A quick follow-up on [topic]"

STRUCTURE:

[HOOK — 1 sentence]
Open with a specific, counterintuitive insight related to their guide topic.
Something that makes them pause and think: "I hadn't considered that."

[THE POINT — 3–4 sentences]
Deliver one concrete, useful idea. Related to the guide but standalone.
Think of it as a bonus insight that didn't fit in the PDF.
Real, specific, actionable — not a tease. Not "to learn more, sign up."

[BRIDGE — 1 sentence]
A natural connection to ETKM's approach without selling.
"This is exactly the kind of thing we dig into in training."

[SIGN-OFF]
Same warm, personal format as A1.

NO CTA. Pure value. This is still the dating phase.
```

---

### Touch A3 — Day 4

**Job:** Dismantle a false belief through a student story. This is the most important email in the sequence.
**Channel:** Email
**Length:** Medium-long. 150–200 words.

```
SUBJECT LINE OPTIONS:
— "She almost didn't come"
— "He told us he was too old"
— "What [Student First Name] believed before she started"
— "The thing that almost stopped [Name] from training"

STRUCTURE:

[THE STORY OPENING — 1–2 sentences]
Introduce the student immediately. Real name (or first name + initial if preferred).
Relatable descriptor. The specific false belief they held.
"[Name] came to us convinced she was too out of shape to start."

[THE HESITATION — 1–2 sentences]
Name the false belief as it felt from the inside.
Don't dismiss it. Validate why it felt true.
"And honestly? It made sense. She hadn't worked out in years.
 She pictured a room full of people who'd been training since college."

[THE TURN — 1 sentence]
What changed. Simple. Specific.
"She came to her trial lesson anyway."

[THE SHIFT — 2–3 sentences]
What happened. What she noticed. How she felt after.
Focus on identity shift — not just physical results.
"In the first 20 minutes, she learned a defense she'd never seen before — 
 and she could actually do it. By the time class ended, something had shifted.
 She signed up that afternoon."

[THE BRIDGE — 1–2 sentences]
Connect to the prospect without pressure.
"[Name]'s story isn't unusual here. It's what we see again and again."

[SOFT CTA]
First CTA in the sequence. Keep it low-pressure.
"If you're curious what that first class is actually like, I'm happy to answer 
 any questions before you come in."

Reply-to CTA is often more effective here than a link. Lowers friction.
Makes the conversation feel personal, not automated.
```

---

### Touch A4 — Day 7

**Job:** First direct invitation. Warm but clear.
**Channel:** Email + optional SMS
**Length:** Short. 80–100 words.

```
SUBJECT LINE OPTIONS:
— "When you're ready — here's the next step"
— "Your first class is on us, [First Name]"
— "One step, [First Name]"

STRUCTURE:

[REFERENCE BACK — 1 sentence]
Briefly acknowledge the journey so far. Light touch.
"It's been about a week since you downloaded [Guide Name]."

[THE INVITATION — 2–3 sentences]
Clear, warm, zero pressure.
"We'd love for you to come in and experience a class for yourself.
 The trial lesson is completely free — no commitment, no contract.
 Just come see what training actually feels like."

[THE OFFER — 1 sentence]
Name the primary CTA clearly and simply.
**"Schedule your free trial lesson here: [link]"**

[WHAT TO EXPECT — 1–2 sentences]
Remove a logistics fear. What does "free trial lesson" actually mean?
"You'll train with the group, meet the instructor, and leave knowing
 whether this is right for you."

[SIGN-OFF]
Warm. No pressure language. No urgency tricks.
"Hope to see you on the mat.
 — [Instructor Name]"
```

---

### Touch A5 — Day 9

**Job:** Deliver a core training insight that creates desire through education.
**Channel:** Email
**Length:** Medium. 100–150 words.

```
SUBJECT LINE OPTIONS:
— "The real reason people freeze"
— "Why awareness is a trainable skill"
— "What training actually changes"

STRUCTURE:

[THE INSIGHT — 1 sentence hook]
A specific, grounded training concept that is genuinely interesting.
Related to one of ETKM's six core themes: Mindset, Awareness, Simplicity,
Drilling, Proven System, or Evolving with Threats.

[THE EXPLANATION — 3–4 sentences]
Teach it plainly. No jargon without definition. Real and specific.
"Here's why [concept] matters more than most people think..."

[THE APPLICATION — 1–2 sentences]
What does this mean for the prospect personally?
Connects the insight to their world, their situation, their goal.

[SOFT CTA — optional]
Only if natural. Don't force it.
"This is one of the first things we cover in training.
 If you haven't scheduled your trial yet — [link]."

[SIGN-OFF]
```

---

### Touch A6 — Day 11

**Job:** Social proof. Let students speak.
**Channel:** Email
**Length:** Short. 80–100 words + testimonial.

```
SUBJECT LINE OPTIONS:
— "What [City/East Texas] students are saying"
— "In their words"
— "[Student Name]'s experience at ETKM"

STRUCTURE:

[BRIEF INTRO — 1–2 sentences]
Set up the testimonial. Don't over-explain.
"Sometimes the best way to know if something is right for you is to hear 
 from someone who was in your exact position."

[THE TESTIMONIAL]
1 full testimonial. Real name. Specific. Identity-focused.
Must follow the before → hesitation → shift → after arc.
Select based on the prospect's persona — match the student to the lead.

[BRIDGE — 1 sentence]
"[Name]'s experience is typical — not exceptional."

[CTA]
Direct. Clear.
**"Schedule your free trial: [link]"**

[SIGN-OFF]
```

---

### Touch A7 — Day 14

**Job:** Final invitation. Clear, warm, clean close.
**Channel:** Email
**Length:** Very short. 60–80 words.

```
SUBJECT LINE OPTIONS:
— "Last thing I'll send for a while, [First Name]"
— "Leaving the door open"
— "One more time — then I'll let you be"

STRUCTURE:

[HONEST OPENER — 1 sentence]
Acknowledge this is the last touch in the sequence.
"I don't want to fill your inbox — so this is the last email in this series."

[THE DOOR LEFT OPEN — 2–3 sentences]
No pressure. No urgency theater. Just genuine.
"If now isn't the right time, that's completely fine.
 Whenever you're ready — whether that's next week or next year — 
 the trial lesson is always free and the door is always open."

[FINAL CTA]
**"[link] — Schedule your trial lesson whenever you're ready."**

[SIGN-OFF]
"Take care of yourself out there.
 — [Instructor Name]"

NOTE: After A7, prospect moves to long-term nurture — 
weekly/biweekly value content with no hard CTA pressure.
```

## Sequence B — Seminar Registration Nurture

**Entry trigger:** Prospect registers for a free safety seminar (fightbacketx.com or ETKM).
**Goal:** Maximize attendance. Deliver value at the event. Follow up to convert.

---

### Touch B1 — Immediate (Registration Confirmation)

```
SUBJECT: "You're registered — here's what to know"

STRUCTURE:
- Confirm registration (date, time, location — dead simple)
- 1–2 sentences on what they'll learn and why it's worth showing up
- What to bring / what to wear (remove friction)
- Warm sign-off
- NO additional selling

LENGTH: Very short. This is logistics, not a pitch.
```

---

### Touch B2 — 48 Hours Before

```
SUBJECT: "See you [Day], [First Name]"

STRUCTURE:
- Simple reminder: date/time/location
- One sentence of genuine anticipation (not hype)
- One short student-voice quote about what surprised them at their first class
- "If you have any questions before Saturday, just reply to this email."
- NO selling

LENGTH: Short.
```

---

### Touch B3 — Morning of Event

```
SUBJECT: "Today's the day"

STRUCTURE:
- 3–4 sentences max. That's it.
- Warm. Calm. "We're looking forward to meeting you."
- Final logistics reminder (arrive 10 min early / what to wear)
- NO selling. Zero.

LENGTH: Extremely short. This is a warm handshake, not a message.
```

---

### Touch B4 — 24 Hours After Event

```
SUBJECT: "Good to meet you, [First Name]"

STRUCTURE:
- Reference something specific from the event if possible
- 2–3 sentences acknowledging what they experienced
- Bridge to the trial lesson — natural, not aggressive
  "If you want to take what you learned and build on it in a real class, 
   your first session is always free."
- CTA: Schedule trial lesson

LENGTH: Short-medium.
```

---

### Touch B5 — Day 5 Post-Event

```
SUBJECT: "One week out — a quick check-in"

STRUCTURE:
- Acknowledge a week has passed since the seminar
- 1 short student story — someone who came to the seminar and then joined
- Direct, warm CTA: "Your trial lesson is still waiting."
- If no response after this, move to long-term nurture

LENGTH: Short-medium.
```

## Sequence C — Pre-Visit (Trial Lesson Booked)

**Entry trigger:** Prospect has booked their free trial lesson but has not yet attended.
**Goal:** Maximize show-up rate. Eliminate cold feet. Remove every logistics anxiety.
**This is the most conversion-critical sequence in the funnel.**

---

### Touch C1 — Immediately After Booking

```
SUBJECT: "You're all set, [First Name] — here's what to expect"

STRUCTURE:

[CONFIRM THE BOOKING]
Day, time, address. Clear. No buried information.

[WHAT THE FIRST CLASS LOOKS LIKE — 3–4 sentences]
Walk them through what actually happens when they arrive.
Demystify it. Make it feel approachable.
"When you arrive, [Instructor Name] will meet you personally before class starts.
 You'll warm up with the group, learn a core technique, and drill it with a partner.
 Most people leave feeling surprised by how quickly things clicked."

[WHAT TO BRING / WEAR]
One line. Simple.
"Wear comfortable athletic clothing. Water bottle recommended. Nothing else required."

[THE OPEN DOOR]
"If you have any questions before your first class — about what we cover, 
 who trains with us, or anything else — just reply to this email."

[SIGN-OFF]
"Looking forward to meeting you.
 — [Instructor Name]"

NO SELLING. This email is pure logistics + warmth.
```

---

### Touch C2 — 24 Hours Before

```
SUBJECT: "See you tomorrow, [First Name]"

STRUCTURE:

[REMINDER — 1 sentence]
Day, time, address. One sentence only.

[THE TRUTH ABOUT FIRST CLASSES — 2–3 sentences]
Address the #1 pre-visit fear without naming it directly.
"Almost everyone who walks in for the first time tells us the same thing afterward: 
 'I didn't know what to expect — but it wasn't what I was nervous about.'
 You're going to do great."

[STUDENT QUOTE]
One short quote. Real student. First-class experience.
Select a quote that addresses the most common pre-visit anxiety.

[LOGISTICS REMINDER]
One line. Quick.

[SIGN-OFF]
Warm and short.

NO SELLING.
```

---

### Touch C3 — Morning of Trial Lesson

```
SUBJECT: "Today, [First Name]"

STRUCTURE:
4 sentences max. That is the entire email.

"Today's your day. We're looking forward to meeting you.
 [Time, Address] — come a few minutes early so we can say hello before class.
 See you on the mat. — [Instructor Name]"

Nothing else. The brevity is intentional and effective.
```

---

### Touch C3b — No-Show Follow-Up (if they don't attend)

```
SUBJECT: "We missed you today, [First Name]"

STRUCTURE:

[ACKNOWLEDGE — 1 sentence]
"Looks like today didn't work out — completely understood."

[RE-OPEN THE DOOR — 2 sentences]
No guilt. No pressure. Clean and genuine.
"Life gets busy. If you'd like to reschedule, we'd love to have you in.
 Your first trial lesson is still free — no expiration."

[CTA]
Simple link to reschedule.

[SIGN-OFF]
Warm. Brief.

After this, return to Sequence A long-term nurture if no response.
```

## Sequence D — Re-Engagement

**Entry trigger:** Lead has not opened any email in 30+ days.
**Goal:** Either reactivate the relationship or cleanly close the loop.
**Rule:** Three touches only. If no response, move to suppression list.

---

### Touch D1 — Day 1

```
SUBJECT OPTIONS:
— "Still there, [First Name]?"
— "Did we lose you?"
— "Quick check-in"

STRUCTURE:

[HONEST OPENER — 1–2 sentences]
Direct acknowledgment that they've been quiet.
"It's been a while since we've heard from you — and that's completely okay."

[THE RE-OFFER — 2 sentences]
Remind them of the value without recapping everything.
"If the timing wasn't right before, things may look different now.
 Your free trial lesson is still waiting — no pressure, no deadline."

[CTA]
Simple. Direct.

LENGTH: Very short. Brevity signals confidence, not desperation.
```

---

### Touch D2 — Day 4

```
SUBJECT: "One more thought before I go quiet"

STRUCTURE:

[ONE PIECE OF VALUE]
A single insight or story. New content — not a repeat.
Something genuinely useful for their situation.

[THE DOOR]
"If this ever feels like the right time — we're here."
[CTA: Schedule trial]

LENGTH: Short.
```

---

### Touch D3 — Day 7 (Final)

```
SUBJECT: "Closing the loop, [First Name]"

STRUCTURE:

[HONEST CLOSE — 3 sentences max]
"I don't want to keep sending emails that aren't useful to you.
 If now isn't the right time for training — no hard feelings at all.
 We'll be here when the timing is right."

[ONE FINAL CTA]
The link, no pressure framing.

[UNSUBSCRIBE REMINDER]
"If you'd like to stop receiving emails from us, you can unsubscribe below.
 No hard feelings either way."

After D3 with no response → move to suppression list.
Do not continue emailing.
```

## Channel Guidelines — Email vs. SMS

| Touch | Email | SMS | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Immediate delivery (A1, B1, C1) | ✓ Primary | ✓ Optional confirmation | SMS: "Your [Guide Name] is on its way — check your email." |
| Day 2–4 value emails | ✓ Primary | ✗ | Too much SMS too early kills trust |
| First CTA (A4) | ✓ Primary | ✓ Optional | SMS: Short + link only |
| Pre-visit day-of (C3) | ✓ | ✓ Send both | High-stakes show-up moment — use both |
| Re-engagement | ✓ Primary | ✗ | SMS re-engagement feels invasive |

**SMS Rules:**
- Max 160 characters when possible
- Always include first name
- One message = one idea = one link
- Never use SMS for long-form content or stories
- Always include opt-out: "Reply STOP to unsubscribe"

## Sequence Customization by Persona

The sequence structure above is universal. Customize these elements per persona:

| Element | How to Customize |
|---|---|
| Subject lines | Match the emotional register of the persona (parent = protective, commuter = practical) |
| A3 student story | Select a story featuring a student who matches the prospect's persona |
| A5 core insight | Choose the training theme most relevant to the persona's primary pain |
| A6 testimonial | Must come from someone in the same persona category |
| Pre-visit language | Match the anxiety most common for that persona |

## Sequence Performance Standards

Use these benchmarks to evaluate sequence health:

| Metric | Below Average | Target | Strong |
|---|---|---|---|
| Email open rate | < 20% | 25–35% | > 40% |
| Click-through rate | < 2% | 3–6% | > 8% |
| Lead to trial booking | < 5% | 8–15% | > 20% |
| Trial booking to show-up | < 50% | 65–75% | > 80% |
| Trial show-up to member | < 30% | 40–60% | > 65% |

**Troubleshooting by metric:**
- Low open rate → Fix subject lines. Test persona-specific subjects.
- Good opens, low clicks → Fix the CTA. One ask, clear action, lower friction.
- Good clicks, no bookings → Fix the landing page / booking process. Not a sequence problem.
- Good bookings, low show-up → Fix Sequence C (pre-visit). Add more warmth + logistics clarity.
- Good show-up, low conversion → Not a sequence problem. Fix the offer stack and first class experience.

## Quality Checklist — Every Sequence Touch

Before any email goes out, verify:

- [ ] One job only — does this email have a single, clear purpose?
- [ ] Value first — does it give before it asks?
- [ ] Student is the hero — is ETKM positioned as guide, never hero?
- [ ] Internal problem addressed — does it speak to how they *feel*, not just what they *need*?
- [ ] Brand voice — confident, direct, warm, practical? No jargon, no fear-mongering?
- [ ] CTA is clear and singular — one ask, its own line, action language?
- [ ] Length is appropriate — not a word longer than it needs to be?
- [ ] Sign-off is personal — does it feel human, not automated?
- [ ] Subject line creates curiosity or relevance without clickbait?
