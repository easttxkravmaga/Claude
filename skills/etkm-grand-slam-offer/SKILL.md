# ETKM Grand Slam Offer

> Applies the Hormozi $100M Offers framework to ETKM's offers, programs, and events.
> The goal is always the same: make the offer so compelling that a qualified prospect
> feels irrational saying no — without overpromising, without aggressive sales tactics,
> and without violating ETKM's brand voice.

---

## The Core Principle

**Compete on value delivered, not price.**

ETKM will never win a price war with the gym across town. ETKM wins by making the
total perceived value so high that comparing on price becomes absurd. The prospect
should feel like they're getting $500 worth of value for $150/month — because they are.

The framework has one test: *Does this offer make a qualified prospect feel stupid
saying no?* If not, the offer isn't done yet.

---

## The Value Equation — Four Levers

Every offer lives or dies on four variables. To increase offer strength, maximize the
top two and minimize the bottom two.

```
Offer Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement)
              ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
```

| Variable | What It Means for ETKM | How to Move It |
|---|---|---|
| **Dream Outcome** | Feeling safe, confident, capable — protecting the people they love | Make the transformation concrete and specific, not abstract |
| **Perceived Likelihood** | "This will actually work for someone like me" | Testimonials from relatable students, instructor credibility, belt progressions |
| **Time Delay** | How long until they feel results | Emphasize quick wins (first class, first week), name the 90-day transformation |
| **Effort & Sacrifice** | How hard is this going to be? What do I give up? | Flexible schedule, welcoming community, "show up and we handle the rest" |

**Application rule:** Before auditing an offer, score each variable 1–5. The two
lowest scores are where the work goes.

## The Grand Slam Offer — Construction Framework

A Grand Slam Offer is not just a product. It is a **stack** — a core result plus every
element that removes an obstacle standing between the prospect and that result.

### Step 1 — Define the Dream Outcome

State exactly what the prospect gets at the end. Specific. Time-bounded. Relatable.

**ETKM template:**
> "In 90 days, you will know how to protect yourself and your family from a real-world
> threat — or we'll work with you until you do."

**Voice rule:** The outcome is confidence and capability — never dominance, power over
others, or aggression. The dream is *feeling safe* — not *being dangerous*.

---

### Step 2 — Map the Obstacles → Build the Bonuses

The bonus stack is not filler. Each bonus must eliminate one specific objection or fear
that would otherwise stop a prospect from saying yes.

**Process:** List every reason a qualified prospect might hesitate. Then assign a bonus
that directly neutralizes each one.

| Prospect Obstacle | ETKM Bonus That Eliminates It |
|---|---|
| "I don't know if I can do this physically" | Free Private Intro Session — 1-on-1 assessment with Nathan before group class |
| "I'll be behind everyone else and embarrassed" | Beginner-specific on-ramp structure (documented in trial sequence) |
| "I don't have time to study between classes" | Lifetime access to ETKM Student Video Library — review any technique on demand |
| "I don't even know what to wear / bring" | Official Training Uniform included — show up, we handle the rest |
| "What if I'm not ready after the trial?" | Personalized Training Blueprint — written roadmap of exactly where they are and where they're going |
| "I don't know if this actually works in real life" | Curriculum validation: Krav Maga origins, real-world application documented |

**Rule:** Every bonus must have a stated perceived value. Never present a bonus without
anchoring it to what it would cost if purchased separately.

---

### Step 3 — Stack and Name the Core Offer

The full membership offer stack, with perceived values:

| Item | Perceived Value |
|---|---|
| Unlimited Monthly Membership (all classes) | $150/month |
| Official Training Uniform | $80 |
| Lifetime Access — ETKM Student Video Library | $97 |
| Private Intro Session (1-on-1 with Nathan) | $100 |
| Personalized Training Blueprint | $150 |
| Digital Safety Guide *(to be developed)* | $47 |
| **Total Perceived Value** | **$624** |
| **Member Investment** | **$150/month** |

**The conversion math:** $150 against $624 perceived value = prospect pays 24 cents
on the dollar. Price objection disappears.

**When adding new bonuses:** Ask — does this eliminate a real obstacle, or does it just
pad the list? Only the former belongs in the stack.

---

### Step 4 — Build the Guarantee

The guarantee is not a liability. It is a conversion tool. It moves the risk from the
prospect to ETKM — and signals that ETKM is confident in the outcome.

**ETKM guarantee framework — choose by context:**

| Guarantee Type | Language Template | Best Used For |
|---|---|---|
| **Unconditional** | "If you're not satisfied after your first class, we'll refund your trial — no questions asked." | Free trial, first-time prospects |
| **Conditional (outcome-based)** | "Show up consistently for 90 days. If you don't feel meaningfully more confident and capable, we'll extend your membership free until you do." | Core membership conversion |
| **Anti-guarantee (confidence signal)** | "We don't offer refunds — because we've never needed to. But we do offer a free trial so you can decide before you commit." | Strong-brand BOFU close |

**Voice rule:** Guarantees must be calm and certain — not desperate or defensive.
The tone is "we're that confident" — never "please trust us."

---

### Step 5 — Add Scarcity and Urgency (When Appropriate)

**Scarcity** = limited supply (real, never manufactured)
**Urgency** = limited time (real, never manufactured)

ETKM examples:
- Class sizes are capped — enrollment can legitimately close
- Event-based urgency (CBLTAC registration deadline, seminar slots)
- Monthly cohort starts for structured programs

**Rule:** Never manufacture false scarcity. It destroys trust. Only use what's real.

## The MAGIC Naming Formula

Apply to any new offer, event, or program that needs a name.

| Letter | Element | Question to Answer |
|---|---|---|
| **M** | Magnetic reason why | Why this, why now? What makes it worth pausing for? |
| **A** | Avatar | Who specifically is this for? |
| **G** | Goal | What specific result do they get? |
| **I** | Interval | In what timeframe? |
| **C** | Container word | What is it called? (Challenge, Blueprint, System, Bootcamp, Academy) |

**ETKM example application:**

| Element | CBLTAC Event |
|---|---|
| M | One weekend that could change everything |
| A | Armed citizens and law enforcement |
| G | Lethal force decision-making and close-quarter tactics |
| I | 2-day intensive |
| C | Training course |
| **Result** | *2-Day Armed Citizen Tactics Course: Lethal Force Decisions for Everyday Carriers* |

**Voice rule on naming:** No militaristic container words. Avoid: *Warrior, Destroyer,
Combat Machine, Killzone.* Prefer: *Academy, Blueprint, System, Course, Workshop,
Foundation.*

## ETKM Offer Audit Checklist

Run this against any existing or new ETKM offer before it goes live:

- [ ] Is the dream outcome stated specifically and time-bounded?
- [ ] Does each bonus eliminate a real, named obstacle?
- [ ] Does every bonus have an anchored perceived value?
- [ ] Does the total perceived value dwarf the price (target: 3–5x minimum)?
- [ ] Is there a guarantee that removes risk from the prospect?
- [ ] Is scarcity/urgency real — not manufactured?
- [ ] Does the offer name pass the MAGIC formula?
- [ ] Does the offer copy make the student the hero (not ETKM)?
- [ ] Is the primary CTA clear and friction-free?
- [ ] Would a trauma survivor feel safe reading the offer copy?

## Offer-by-Offer Reference — Current ETKM Stack

### Free Trial Lesson (Primary BOFU Entry)
- **Dream Outcome:** Experience one class and know whether ETKM is right for you — zero risk, zero commitment
- **Value Equation bottleneck:** Perceived likelihood (will this work for someone like me?) — address with relatable student testimonials pre-visit
- **Guarantee:** Full refund if unsatisfied after first class
- **Friction to eliminate:** Scheduling friction, "not knowing what to expect," fear of being judged

### Monthly Membership
- **Dream Outcome:** In 90 days, feel confident and capable in real-world scenarios
- **Full stack:** See Step 3 above
- **Primary objection:** Price — neutralized by $624 perceived value stack
- **Guarantee:** Conditional outcome-based (show up 90 days)

### Private Training
- **Dream Outcome:** Specific skill gaps closed, custom progression in less time
- **Pricing principle:** Price on outcome value, not hourly rate
- **Bonus add:** Personalized Training Blueprint included — ties private training to documented progress
- **Urgency:** Limited availability with Nathan directly

### Specialty Events (CBLTAC model)
- **Dream Outcome:** Specific tactical or situational competency gained in one weekend
- **Stack:** Training, materials, certification of completion, community access post-event
- **Scarcity:** Hard seat cap — real, communicable
- **MAGIC naming:** Apply formula for every new event

## Prohibited Outputs

When producing offer copy using this skill, never generate:

- Price-first messaging (always lead with value stack)
- Aggressive or militaristic framing ("destroy threats," "kill or be killed")
- Overpromising ("guaranteed to win any fight")
- Fear-heavy copy that doesn't immediately pivot to capability and solution
- Container words: warrior, killer, destroyer, dominate, crush, elite, beast
- Copy that positions ETKM or Nathan as the hero rather than the student
