# ETKM Lead Generation

> Load etkm-funnel-master before using this skill.
> Lead gen assets live primarily in MOFU — they are the bridge between awareness and conversion.

---

## The Lead Gen Philosophy

A lead magnet is a **value trade**, not a trick.

The prospect gives ETKM their contact information. ETKM gives them something genuinely
useful in return. The asset must be so immediately valuable that they feel good about the trade
— before they've ever walked through the door.

**The lead gen asset does three things:**
1. Delivers real, usable value (builds trust and credibility)
2. Gently positions ETKM as the guide with the answer
3. Opens the door to the nurture sequence

**What a lead magnet is NOT:**
- A brochure about ETKM
- A price list
- A hard sell in disguise
- A watered-down teaser that withholds the real value

## ETKM Primary Lead Gen Format: PDF Guides

PDFs are ETKM's primary digital lead gen format. They are chosen because:
- Low friction to create and distribute
- High perceived value (tangible, downloadable, saveable)
- Easy to tailor to specific personas
- Work across all digital channels (ads, email, social, website)
- Position ETKM as an authority and educator, not just a gym

## PDF Lead Magnet — Master Template Structure

Every ETKM PDF lead magnet follows this structure. The subject matter changes per persona
and campaign. The architecture does not.

---

### SECTION 1 — The Cover

**Elements:**
- ETKM logo (top)
- Headline: Bold, specific, persona-targeted benefit statement
- Subheadline: One sentence that clarifies who this is for and what they'll walk away with
- Hero image or graphic (brand-aligned — see etkm-brand-kit)
- Optional: "A free guide from East Texas Krav Maga"

**Headline Formula:**
`[Number] [Specific Outcome] for [Target Persona] — Even If [Common False Belief]`

Examples (structure only — subject matter TBD):
- "5 Things Every [Persona] Should Know About [Topic] — Even If You've Never Trained Before"
- "The [Persona]'s Guide to [Specific Outcome] in [Timeframe or Context]"
- "[Number] Ways to [Specific Action] — Starting Today"

---

### SECTION 2 — The Hook Page (Page 1 Interior)

**Purpose:** Confirm the reader made the right choice downloading this. Make them feel seen.

**Elements:**
- 2–3 sentence empathy statement that names their specific fear or frustration
- 1 sentence that validates the fear as real and legitimate
- 1 sentence that reframes: "But here's what most people don't know..."
- 1 sentence positioning ETKM as the guide: "That's exactly why we put this together."
- Brief permission statement: What they're about to learn and why it matters

**Voice:** Warm, direct, calm. Not lecture. Not sales. Conversation.

---

### SECTION 3 — The Core Content (2–5 pages)

**Purpose:** Deliver the promised value. This is the real goods.

**Structure per content item:**
- Bold header (the tip, principle, or step)
- 2–4 sentences of explanation
- 1 practical application sentence ("Here's what this looks like in real life...")
- Optional: A brief "most people don't realize" insight that adds depth

**Content rules:**
- Minimum 3 items, maximum 7 items per PDF
- Each item must stand alone as genuinely useful — not dependent on joining ETKM
- Each item should subtly reinforce the protector identity narrative
- No jargon without explanation
- No technique instruction that requires video — this is conceptual, not physical training

**False Belief Dismantle (embed at least one):**
Every PDF should organically address at least one false belief relevant to the persona.
Do not make it a section — weave it into the content naturally.

---

### SECTION 4 — The Bridge (Second-to-Last Page)

**Purpose:** Connect the content to the next step. This is the soft pivot from value to invitation.

**Elements:**
- 1–2 sentences acknowledging what they now know
- 1 sentence that introduces the gap: knowing vs. doing
- 1 sentence that introduces ETKM as the place to close that gap
- Tone: "The next natural step" — never "now buy from us"

**Bridge Formula:**
"Now you know [what the PDF taught]. The question is — what do you do with it?
Knowing is the first step. [ETKM's approach] is where that knowledge becomes real skill.
If you're ready to go from knowing to being ready, here's where to start."

---

### SECTION 5 — The CTA Page (Back Cover)

**Purpose:** One clear, low-friction next step.

**Elements:**
- ETKM logo
- Single CTA headline (not more than one ask)
- 2–3 sentence value statement for the next step
- The CTA itself (bold, specific, action-oriented)
- Website URL
- Optional: QR code linking to the trial lesson booking page

**Primary CTA (Default):**
"Schedule Your Free Trial Lesson" — Always use this unless campaign has a specific
MOFU CTA (e.g., "Register for Our Free Women's Safety Seminar")

**Secondary CTA Option (if prospect is not yet BOFU-ready):**
"Follow us on [platform] for weekly safety tips"
OR
"Join our email list for [specific ongoing value]"

## Opt-In Page Template (Landing Page Copy Structure)

When the PDF is delivered via a landing page, the page copy follows this structure:

**Headline:** Mirror the PDF cover headline
**Subheadline:** Clarify who it's for and what they get
**3-bullet value list:** Three things they'll learn or gain
**Lead capture form:** First name + email (minimum) — phone optional
**Privacy micro-copy:** "We respect your privacy. No spam. Unsubscribe anytime."
**Submit button copy:** NOT "Submit" — use action language:
  - "Send Me the Guide"
  - "Get My Free [Guide Name]"
  - "Yes, I Want This"

## Post-Download Nurture Handoff

When a prospect downloads a PDF, they enter the nurture sequence immediately.
The first follow-up email/text should arrive within 5 minutes of opt-in.

**Nurture sequence structure (skeleton — full sequences built separately):**

| Touch | Timing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Email 1 | Immediate | Deliver the PDF + warm welcome |
| Email 2 | Day 2 | One piece of additional value (related tip) |
| Email 3 | Day 4 | A student story (false belief dismantler) |
| Email 4 | Day 7 | Soft CTA — "When you're ready, here's the next step" |
| Email 5+ | Ongoing | Weekly value content — maintain relationship until ready |

> Full email copy lives in the nurture sequence skill (to be built).

## Quality Check — Every PDF Lead Magnet

Before finalizing any PDF, confirm:

- [ ] Would the prospect find this valuable even if they never joined ETKM?
- [ ] Does it speak directly and specifically to one persona's pain?
- [ ] Does it deliver the promised value — no bait-and-switch?
- [ ] Does it address at least one false belief naturally (not as a hard sell)?
- [ ] Is ETKM positioned as guide — not hero, not salesperson?
- [ ] Is the CTA a natural next step — not an aggressive close?
- [ ] Does it match brand voice (warm, direct, practical)?
- [ ] Is it 6–10 pages (not too short to feel cheap, not too long to feel like homework)?
