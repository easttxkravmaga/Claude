# ETKM Funnel Master

## The Core Philosophy — What Makes ETKM's Funnel Different

ETKM's funnel is not a sales machine. It is a **protector identity pipeline.**

Every stage of the funnel has one job: move someone from *fear and uncertainty* toward
*confidence and belonging*. That transformation — not the techniques, not the price —
is what ETKM is actually selling.

The market doesn't buy self-defense. They buy who they become when they know how to
defend themselves.

**The funnel is a relationship, not a transaction.** Each stage mirrors a stage of trust-building:

| Funnel Stage | Relationship Equivalent | ETKM's Goal |
|---|---|---|
| TOFU — Awareness | Meeting at a party | Stop the scroll. Prove you understand their fear. |
| MOFU — Consideration | Going on dates | Build trust. Dismantle false beliefs. Offer a low-risk next step. |
| BOFU — Decision | The proposal | Make the offer irresistible. Reduce friction. Get the yes. |
| Post-Sale — Retention | The relationship | Deliver quick wins. Build community. Inspire referrals. |

## The Three Funnel Stages — ETKM Definitions

### TOFU — Top of Funnel (Awareness)

**Customer mindset:** Problem-aware, NOT solution-aware.
They are not searching for Krav Maga. They are feeling unsafe, unfit, or underprepared.
They don't know ETKM exists yet.

**ETKM's job at TOFU:**
- Stop the scroll with a hook that speaks directly to their specific pain
- Deliver immediate value (teach them something useful right now)
- Make them aware ETKM is the guide who understands their world
- Do NOT sell anything. Do NOT ask for anything.

**What success looks like at TOFU:**
- They watch the video / read the post all the way through
- They follow the account or save the content
- They click through to learn more
- Metric: Reach, impressions, new unique visitors, video views

**ETKM TOFU Principles:**
- Hooks must be persona-specific (see Persona Matrix below)
- Content delivers real, usable value — not teasers
- Fear is acknowledged calmly and immediately followed by capability
- ETKM's brand voice: confident, warm, practical (never aggressive or fear-mongering)

---

### MOFU — Middle of Funnel (Consideration)

**Customer mindset:** Solution-aware. Now comparing options and evaluating fit.
They know Krav Maga exists. They are asking: "Is this for me? Can I actually do this?
Is ETKM the right place?"

**ETKM's job at MOFU:**
- Tell the story that creates emotional connection (Epiphany Bridge)
- Dismantle false beliefs (see False Belief Library below)
- Compare ETKM graciously to alternatives — frame the right choice for the right person
- Capture contact information via low-friction lead magnet
- Begin the nurture sequence

**What success looks like at MOFU:**
- They download the PDF / register for the seminar / opt in to the list
- They engage with email/text follow-up
- They start asking questions
- Metric: Lead capture rate, email open rate, seminar attendance, engagement

**ETKM MOFU Principles:**
- Student is always the hero — never feature elite athletes or intimidating skill demos
- Feature relatable transformation stories (soccer mom, 50-year-old accountant, commuter)
- Comparison content is honest and gracious — never aggressive toward competitors
- The free seminar (fightbacketx.com model) is ETKM's highest-performing MOFU entry point
- PDF lead magnets are the primary digital MOFU entry point (see etkm-lead-gen skill)

---

### BOFU — Bottom of Funnel (Decision)

**Customer mindset:** Ready to buy. Choosing WHERE to buy.
They've decided they want self-defense training. Now they're comparing ETKM vs. the
school across town, looking at reviews, checking pricing, considering whether to actually
show up.

**ETKM's job at BOFU:**
- Present the offer stack (not just a price — a value bundle)
- Make transparent pricing easy to find
- Lead with social proof and transformation testimonials
- Reduce every source of friction
- Create urgency without being manipulative

**What success looks like at BOFU:**
- They schedule the Free Trial Lesson
- They show up
- Metric: Conversion rate, trial lesson bookings, trial-to-member conversion

**ETKM BOFU Principles:**
- Primary CTA is always "Schedule Your Free Trial Lesson" — low friction, high value
- The offer stack must make $150/month feel negligible compared to total perceived value
- Transparent pricing on the page — no "call for pricing" friction
- Video testimonials showing relatable students (not elite athletes)
- Attach story to physical items (gloves, uniform) to elevate perceived value

---

### Post-Sale — Retention & Ascension

**Customer mindset:** Newly committed. Uncertain if they made the right call.
The purchase decision is fresh. The first class is everything.

**ETKM's job post-sale:**
- Deliver a quick win in the first 10 minutes of the first class
- Execute the 90-day onboarding sequence (see etkm-onboarding skill when built)
- Build community belonging fast
- Engineer the referral engine
- Guide ascension up the value ladder

**Value Ladder (ETKM Ascension Path):**
| Stage | Offer | Approx. Value |
|---|---|---|
| Entry | Free Trial Lesson | — |
| Core | Monthly Membership | $150/month |
| Upgrade | Black Belt Club | $200/month |
| Premium | Instructor Certification | ~$2,000 |
| Ongoing | Specialized seminars / workshops | Variable |

## False Belief Library

These are the silent killers of ETKM conversions. MOFU content must address these
without being confrontational. Always dismantle through story and social proof — never
through lecture or argument.

| False Belief | The Truth | Content Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| "I need to get in shape before I start" | Krav Maga IS how you get in shape | Feature before/after transformation stories from out-of-shape starters |
| "I'm too old to start this" | ETKM has students 50+ thriving | Feature 50+ student stories prominently |
| "I'm not aggressive enough" | Krav Maga is about technique and leverage, not aggression | Show calm, measured instruction — highlight non-aggressive students succeeding |
| "It won't work in a real situation" | Krav Maga is built exclusively for real situations, not sport | Show scenario-based training clips, instructor credibility signals |
| "I don't have time" | Classes fit around real schedules | Show schedule flexibility, time-efficient training clips |
| "It's too expensive" | Addressed by the offer stack at BOFU | Don't address at TOFU/MOFU — build value first, price comes last |
| "I'll be judged / embarrassed" | Community is welcoming and non-judgmental | Feature warm community moments, new student stories |

## Funnel Troubleshooting Guide

When the funnel isn't working, don't guess — diagnose by behavior:

| Symptom | Broken Stage | Root Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ads get clicks but no leads | MOFU | Story isn't connecting / false beliefs not addressed | Improve nurture content, add relatable stories |
| No clicks on ads/content | TOFU | Hook is weak — not stopping the scroll | Redesign hook to match persona pain point |
| Leads don't show up for trial | MOFU → BOFU gap | Not enough nurture between opt-in and ask | Add bridge content between lead capture and CTA |
| Trial lesson booked but no-show | BOFU | Friction or cold feet — not enough social proof | Add pre-visit text/email sequence with testimonials |
| Trial attended but didn't convert | BOFU | Offer isn't compelling enough | Strengthen offer stack, add urgency |
| Members quit early | Post-Sale | No quick win in first class / no onboarding | Fix time-to-first-value experience |

## Key Rules for All Funnel Work

1. **Never propose at the party.** Don't sell at TOFU. Don't even hint at price. Give value.
2. **The student is always the hero.** ETKM is the guide. Every stage must reflect this.
3. **Build trust before asking for anything.** Each stage earns the right to ask for the next thing.
4. **False beliefs kill conversions silently.** MOFU content must address them through story, not argument.
5. **Price is always last.** Build perceived value in layers before price is ever mentioned.
6. **The funnel is nonlinear.** Prospects loop, pause, and re-enter. Build for patience and consistency.
7. **Story is the product.** The transformation story is what converts — more than any feature or technique.
