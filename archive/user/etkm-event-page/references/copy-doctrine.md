## The One Truth This System Is Built On

**"Bad decisions don't come from lack of skill — they come from unmanaged stress."**

This is the controlling insight. It is John Wilson's line. It goes on every page, in every variant.
Never dilute it. Never bury it. It is the engine that makes the whole page work.

---

## Headline System

### H1 Structure (Hero)
Three-line pattern with red on the PAYOFF line — not the setup line.
- Line 1: Setup (white)
- Line 2: **RED** — the emotional hit
- Line 3: Resolution (white) — optional

Examples:
- Train to Think Clearly / **WHEN IT MATTERS MOST** — general
- Your Decisions / **UNDER PRESSURE** / Define Your Career — professional
- You Handle Crises / **FOR EVERYONE ELSE.** / Who Handles It for You? — first responder

Rule: Red carries the cost or the tension. White carries the promise or the question.

### Section Headlines
Short. Aggressive. Two lines max. Sentence case or ALL CAPS — never mixed.
- "Two Courses. One Complete System."
- "Built for the People Who Run Toward the Problem."
- "He Didn't Build This From a Textbook."
- "Real People. Real Pressure. **Real Results.**"

### Final CTA Headlines
Three-line structure. Red lands on the most powerful word or phrase.
- "Train More. / **Fear Less.**"
- "Your Judgment Is / **Your Greatest Asset.** / Protect It."
- "You Give Everything / **On Every Shift.** / This One's for You."

## Body Copy Rules

**Sentence length:** Short. Direct. Active voice. No hedging.
- ✓ "This training teaches you to interrupt that process."
- ✗ "This training is designed to potentially help you interrupt that process."

**Empathy before authority.** Acknowledge their reality first, then introduce the solution.

**Never fear-monger.** State the danger calmly, then move immediately to the plan.

**Never over-promise.** No "guaranteed outcomes" or "you'll never freeze again."

**The "wisdom" line** (keep verbatim in every format block):
> "You can observe, learn, and absorb just as much by watching skilled people work through the material together. Some call that keeping your distance. We call it wisdom."

## Who-Grid Card Copy Formula

Each card: 1–2 sentences.
- Sentence 1: Acknowledge their specific reality/risk.
- Sentence 2: Connect it to why this training matters to them specifically.

Length: 20–35 words per card. Not a list. Not bullet points. Prose.

## Application Block Labels

Format: ALL CAPS, red, tight.
- "LAW ENFORCEMENT & SECURITY"
- "SALES & NEGOTIATION"
- "LEADERS UNDER PRESSURE"

Text below: 1 sentence. Specific. Outcome-focused.
- ✓ "Stay composed during critical incidents when the outcome depends on your clarity."
- ✗ "Learn how to stay calm during difficult situations."

## Event Bar Copy

Short. Factual. No filler.
- Label: uppercase, small, gray
- Value: white, readable size
- Format: `[DAY] · [TIME] · $[PRICE]`

---

## Instructor Bio Rules

- **General/Professional variant:** Lead with "founder of CBLTAC" — position expertise first, background second
- **First Responder variant:** Lead with "20 years as a California Highway Patrol officer" — credentials first
- Always include: "This is his third event in East Texas. The demand to bring him back speaks for itself."
- Always include the "wish I had this" quote (it's his — use it)
- Never inflate. His credentials are extraordinary on their own.

---

## Pricing Copy

- Display prices in large Barlow Condensed — the number IS the visual
- "Best Value" label on the both-sessions option always
- Discounted pricing: show original with strikethrough + discounted in red
- Savings callout: "Save $[X]" in red, small, below the price
- Note text: "Per Person · Single Session" or "Per Person · Full Day" — lowercase, gray

## Prohibited Copy

Never use on event pages:
- "mastery" / "dominate" / "destroy" / "warrior" / "elite" / "beast" / "crush"
- "killer instincts" or any violence-framing
- "guaranteed to [outcome]"
- Fear-heavy copy without an immediate plan/solution
- Specific year count for Nathan's experience (use "decades of experience")
- Jargon without explanation (e.g., "stress inoculation" should be paired with plain language)

## What Makes This Copy Different

This is not self-defense marketing. It is not tactical gear language. It is not fear-porn.

It speaks to people who are already competent — and shows them why competence alone fails under stress. The message is: *You are capable. Stress has been stripping that capability away. Here is what to do about it.*

That framing is what makes the CBLTAC pages work across audiences ranging from C-suite executives to paramedics to parents. The problem is universal. The solution is the same. The framing adapts.
