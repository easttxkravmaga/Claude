# ETKM Leads Engine

> Applies Hormozi's $100M Leads framework to ETKM's lead generation system.
> The goal is a repeatable lead machine — predictable inflow of engaged prospects
> that feeds the trial lesson pipeline without requiring heroic founder effort every week.

---

## The Core Distinction: Leads vs. Engaged Leads

**A lead is a contact record. An engaged lead is a person who has actively signaled interest.**

Most gyms and martial arts schools measure vanity metrics — followers, impressions,
website visits. None of those pay the rent. ETKM's real output metric is:

> **Engaged Lead = a contactable person who has voluntarily signaled interest in
> self-defense training or a related ETKM offer.**

### ETKM Engaged Lead Definitions — by action:

| Action | Engaged Lead? | Quality Level |
|---|---|---|
| Downloads a PDF lead magnet | ✅ Yes | Medium — top of funnel |
| Registers for a free seminar (fightbacketx.com) | ✅ Yes | High — warm intent |
| DMs asking about classes or pricing | ✅ Yes | High — hot intent |
| Books a Free Trial Lesson | ✅ Yes | Very High — BOFU ready |
| Comments on a post | ❌ Not yet | Attention only |
| Follows the account | ❌ Not yet | Attention only |
| Visits the website | ❌ Not yet | Attention only |

**Rule:** Never optimize for followers or impressions. Optimize for the actions above.
If a campaign doesn't move someone into one of those engaged states, it's awareness
spend — not lead generation.

## The Core Four — ETKM Channel Map

Hormozi identifies four irreducible methods for generating leads. Every acquisition
tactic maps to one of these. ETKM runs all four — sequenced by maturity and ROI.

```
                    WARM AUDIENCE          COLD AUDIENCE
                 ┌─────────────────┬─────────────────────┐
ONE-TO-ONE       │  Warm Outreach  │    Cold Outreach     │
                 │   (Channel 1)   │     (Channel 3)      │
                 ├─────────────────┼─────────────────────┤
ONE-TO-MANY      │  Free Content   │      Paid Ads        │
                 │   (Channel 2)   │     (Channel 4)      │
                 └─────────────────┴─────────────────────┘
```

**Sequencing rule (Hormozi's recommended order):**
Start with Warm Outreach → build Free Content → add Cold Outreach → run Paid Ads
once the offer is proven and economics are trackable.

---

### Channel 1 — Warm Outreach

**What it is:** Systematic, direct contact with existing relationships and warm connections
— students, former students, community contacts, referral partners, anyone who already
knows Nathan or ETKM.

**Why it works:** Trust is borrowed from existing history. Conversion rate is 5–10x
higher than cold. Fastest path to trial lesson bookings.

**ETKM warm outreach targets:**
- Current active students (referral ask at belt promotions, milestone moments)
- Former students who dropped off (re-engagement)
- Community contacts — church members, employers, coaches Nathan knows personally
- Connectors — pastors, HR managers, youth coaches who know ETKM exists
- Anyone who ever visited the website, commented, or expressed interest but didn't convert

**ETKM warm outreach system:**
- Script: personal, conversational, not salesy — "I thought of you when..." or "We have
  something new that might be a fit for someone you know..."
- Batching: set a daily outreach target (5–10 contacts/day) and hold it consistently
- Ask who they know: after every conversation, "Is there anyone in your life who's been
  thinking about this?"
- Follow-up: most warm leads convert on follow-up 2 or 3, not on first contact

**Primary output:** Trial lesson bookings and referral introductions.

---

### Channel 2 — Free Content

**What it is:** Consistent posting of educational, proof-based, and story-driven content
that earns attention from warm and cold audiences and routes that attention into
engaged lead actions (PDF download, seminar registration, DM, trial booking).

**The rule:** Content is not self-expression. It is a lead generation asset.
Every piece of content must have a conversion layer — a CTA that moves the viewer
toward an engaged lead action.

**ETKM content system:**

| Content Type | Purpose | Primary CTA |
|---|---|---|
| Technique demonstration | Proof — this works and is learnable | "Start with a Free Trial" |
| Student transformation story | Social proof — relatable hero | "Book your first class" |
| Situational awareness tip | Value — teaches something immediately useful | "Download the Safety Guide" |
| "Myth vs. reality" post | Belief dismantling — kills false objections | "See for yourself — Free Trial" |
| Behind-the-scenes / community | Trust / warmth — ETKM is welcoming | "Come see us — Free Trial" |
| PEACE framework content | Identity aspiration — who they're becoming | Seminar registration or Free Trial |

**Content CTA hierarchy:**
1. Book a Free Trial Lesson (highest intent ask — use for warm-signal audiences)
2. Register for free seminar via fightbacketx.com (medium ask — awareness audiences)
3. Download the PDF lead magnet (lowest friction — cold audiences)

**Cadence rule:** Consistency beats perfection. 3–5 posts/week every week outperforms
burst campaigns followed by silence. Content compounds — older posts keep generating
leads as long as the account is active.

**Content to lead funnel:** Every 10 pieces of content should have at least 1 post
driving directly to a lead magnet or trial booking CTA. Track opt-ins and DMs per
10-post cycle to measure conversion rate.

---

### Channel 3 — Cold Outreach

**What it is:** Targeted, direct outreach to people who don't know ETKM yet — but fit
a specific audience profile — through DMs, email, or in-person contact.

**The rule:** Cold outreach isn't dead — it's usually just lazy. Relevance, directness,
and risk reduction are the levers. A cold message must earn its read in the first
sentence.

**ETKM cold outreach targets:**
- Local employers (HR contacts) — employee safety and wellness programs
- Churches and community organizations — women's safety seminars, family programs
- Local schools and youth sports organizations — youth program and teen safety
- First responder departments — EFC and law enforcement training programs
- New residents in Tyler TX — people who just moved and haven't found community yet

**ETKM cold outreach structure:**
1. Specific reason why this person/organization specifically (not a mass-blast)
2. One-sentence description of the relevant offer
3. Low-friction next step (a conversation, not a commitment)
4. Follow-up sequence of 3–5 touches before moving on

**Primary output:** Partnership conversations, employer program discussions,
community seminar bookings.

---

### Channel 4 — Paid Ads

**What it is:** Structured, paid distribution of content and lead magnet offers to
targeted cold audiences on Facebook and Instagram.

**The rule:** Ads are math and iteration, not inspiration. The goal is profitable
cost per engaged lead. Do not run paid ads until the offer is proven and you can
track what happens after the click.

**ETKM paid ads pre-conditions:**
- [ ] Lead magnet landing page is live and tracking opt-ins
- [ ] Follow-up sequence is active after opt-in
- [ ] Free Trial booking page is live and tracking bookings
- [ ] You can answer: "What does one engaged lead cost me, and what is it worth?"

**ETKM ad structure (Hormozi's framework):**
- Hook: persona-specific problem statement (stops the scroll)
- Proof: one relatable student story or instructor credential
- Offer: the lead magnet or free trial (not a membership sale — too much friction)
- CTA: one action only — download, register, or book

**Testing cadence:** 3 creatives × 2 angles × 2 audiences minimum before drawing
conclusions. Run with enough volume — tiny tests produce false negatives.

**Scaling rule — More/Better/New (see below):** Don't add platforms until you've
exhausted volume on Facebook/Instagram.

## Lead Magnets — The Salty Pretzel Principle

Hormozi's frame: a lead magnet is a **complete solution to a narrow problem** that
creates demand for the larger solution. It must be valuable enough to want, easy enough
to say yes to, and specific enough to attract the right person.

The metaphor: salty pretzels make you thirsty. The lead magnet creates the appetite
for the core offer.

**ETKM lead magnet criteria:**
- Solves one specific fear or problem completely (not a teaser)
- Attracts the right audience segment (persona-matched)
- Delivers immediate value — the prospect is better off after consuming it
- Creates awareness of a gap that ETKM's core program solves
- Has a clear CTA to the next step (trial, seminar, or conversation)

**ETKM current and priority lead magnets:**

| Lead Magnet | Persona | Narrow Problem Solved | Gap Revealed | CTA |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Women's Safety Seminar (fightbacketx.com) | Adult women — safety | What to actually do in common threat scenarios | "I know what to do but not how to do it under pressure" | Enroll in training |
| PDF Safety Guide *(to be built)* | Parents / commuters | Situation-specific awareness checklist | "I'm not trained to act on what I notice" | Free Trial Lesson |
| Free Trial Lesson | All warm audiences | Experience real training risk-free | "This is real and I want more" | Membership enrollment |
| Private Training Blueprint | Private training prospects | Understand exactly where they are and what they need | "I need a structured path, not just open mats" | Private training package |

**Lead magnet design rule:** If the lead magnet could be sold as a standalone product,
it's good enough to be a lead magnet.

---

## Lead Magnets — Diagnosis as Engagement Tool

Hormozi's insight: **diagnosis creates urgency**. When you reveal a prospect's specific
gap (current state vs. desired state) and the cost of that gap, they self-qualify and
self-motivate.

**ETKM diagnostic tools:**
- The Private Training Blueprint is already a diagnostic asset — it reveals the gap
  between where the student is and where they need to be
- A free "Safety Assessment" conversation (low-commitment call or in-person) can serve
  the same function for cold leads who aren't ready to book a trial
- Social content framed as "Can you do X under pressure?" is a soft diagnostic that
  creates self-awareness and drives trial bookings

## More / Better / New — The Scaling Loop

Once a channel is working, use this heuristic to grow it. Choose **one lever per
week** — not all three simultaneously.

| Lever | What It Means | ETKM Application |
|---|---|---|
| **More** | Increase volume of actions already working | More outreach contacts/day, more posts/week, higher ad budget on winning creative |
| **Better** | Improve conversion, targeting, or creative | Tighten lead magnet CTA, improve follow-up speed, test new hook angles |
| **New** | Expand to new platforms, placements, or audiences | Add YouTube, expand to new Tyler TX zip codes in ads, test new persona segments |

**Rule:** Never jump to New before exhausting More and Better on current channels.
The most common mistake is constant platform-hopping instead of compounding volume
on what works.

## Lead Getters — Leverage Beyond Nathan

A lead machine that depends entirely on Nathan's personal effort isn't a machine —
it's a job. Lead Getters are systems and people that generate leads without requiring
Nathan to do all the work personally.

### Lead Getter 1 — Current Students (Referral Engine)

Students are ETKM's highest-quality lead source. Referrals convert at 2–3x the rate
of cold leads and arrive pre-sold on the culture.

**System requirements:**
- Ask at the right moment: belt promotions, milestone achievements, strong class
  experiences — not randomly
- Make the ask specific: "Who in your life has been talking about wanting to feel safer?
  I'd love to offer them a free trial on your behalf."
- Incentivize (optional): training credit, gear, or recognition for successful referrals
- Track: every referral source logged in Pipedrive — measure which students refer most
  and why

**Current ETKM status:** Referrals are ETKM's strongest conversion channel. The system
is informal. Goal: formalize the ask cadence and track it in Pipedrive.

### Lead Getter 2 — Community Partners (Affiliates / Partners)

Organizations that reach ETKM's target audiences — churches, employers, women's groups,
youth sports organizations — can distribute ETKM's lead magnets to their members.

**The affiliate principle:** Make the offer so good for the partner that they'd feel
stupid not to share it. The partner gets to add value to their community. ETKM gets
warm, pre-qualified leads.

**ETKM partner targets:**
- Churches (women's safety seminars, family programs)
- Local employers (HR wellness programs, lunch-and-learn safety training)
- Youth sports organizations (youth program, parent safety awareness)
- Real estate agents and property managers (new resident safety awareness)
- Healthcare employers (nurses, hospital staff — high-risk commuters)

**Partner enablement:** Give partners a simple, done-for-you asset package — flyer,
email template, social post — so sharing requires zero effort on their part.

### Lead Getter 3 — Content Compounding

Published content is a passive lead getter. A video or post from 6 months ago can
still generate leads today. The goal is building a content library that works 24/7.

ETKM's content should be designed for longevity — evergreen technique posts, student
story videos, and safety education content that remain relevant indefinitely.

### Lead Getter 4 — Agencies (When Ready)

When ETKM is ready to scale paid ads beyond what Nathan can manage directly, a
digital ad agency can compress the learning curve. The rule: use agencies to learn,
not to avoid learning. Understand what they're doing so the capability stays in-house.

## Open-to-Goal Execution

Hormozi's execution principle: **set a measurable output target and do whatever actions
are required to hit it every day.** Don't manage effort — manage outcomes.

**ETKM daily/weekly output targets (suggested starting point):**

| Output | Daily Target | Weekly Target |
|---|---|---|
| Engaged leads (any channel) | 1–2/day | 7–10/week |
| Warm outreach contacts | 5–10/day | — |
| Content pieces published | — | 3–5/week |
| Trial lesson bookings | — | 3–5/week |
| Referral asks made | 1–2/day | — |
| Partner outreach contacts | — | 3–5/week |

**Rule:** If the targets aren't being hit, don't adjust the target — diagnose the
channel. Is it a volume problem (do more), a conversion problem (make it better),
or a channel problem (try something new)?

## Decision Rules

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| Fewer than 20 warm prospects in pipeline | Start with warm outreach — nothing else first |
| Low conversion on trial bookings | Fix the offer/lead magnet messaging before scaling traffic |
| Running paid ads with no tracking | Stop. Install tracking before spending another dollar |
| Can't describe ETKM's offer in one sentence | You don't have an ad problem — you have a clarity problem |
| Slow response to inquiries (>1 hour) | Fix speed-to-lead before generating more leads |
| Platform-hopping to find "better" audiences | Go back to More/Better on current platform first |
| Good engaged lead volume but low trial conversions | Offer problem, not lead problem — load etkm-grand-slam-offer |

## ETKM Lead Machine — 30-Day Starter Sequence

**Week 1:** Define ETKM's engaged lead (done above). Confirm lead magnet is live and
tracking. Confirm follow-up sequence is active. Set daily output targets.

**Week 2:** Warm outreach blitz — 5–10 contacts/day. Start content cadence — 3–5
posts/week, each with a CTA. Make referral asks after every class.

**Week 3:** Cold outreach to 3–5 community partners. Tighten follow-up scripts based
on response patterns. Add or improve one lead magnet.

**Week 4:** Run small paid ad tests only if tracking is in place. Begin partner
enablement — create the asset package that makes sharing easy for partners.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Measuring followers and impressions instead of engaged leads
- Running paid ads to a vague offer with no lead magnet
- Posting content with no CTA (content without conversion is entertainment, not marketing)
- Under-testing paid ads — too small budget, too short duration
- Confusing a sales/offer problem with an advertising problem
- No systematic follow-up (speed-to-lead matters — first responder wins)
- Platform-hopping before volume is exhausted on current channels
- Building a referral program but never making a direct ask
