# ETKM PEACE Framework

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-09

---

## What PEACE Is

PEACE is ETKM's transformation framework. It maps the identity shift
a student undergoes from prospect to protector. It is derived from
Donald Miller's StoryBrand methodology and adapted specifically for
ETKM's training philosophy and brand voice.

PEACE is not a slogan. It is the structure underneath the slogans.
Every letter represents a stage of transformation, and each stage
has its own messaging language, emotional register, and content purpose.

---

## The Five Pillars

### P — PREPARED

**Identity state:** I have a plan. I am not winging it.

**Core message:** Hope is not a strategy. Preparation replaces panic.

**Slogans:**
- Hope is not a strategy. Preparation is.
- Preparation replaces panic.
- Readiness is a choice made early.
- You do not rise to the moment — you fall to preparation.

**Content role:** Early-stage awareness content (TOFU). Exposes the
gap between where the prospect is and where they need to be. Challenges
the assumption that "it won't happen to me."

**Emotional register:** Wake-up call. Not fear — clarity.

---

### E — EMPOWERED

**Identity state:** I have the tools and the right to use them.

**Core message:** Empowerment begins with agency, not permission.

**Slogans:**
- Empowerment begins with agency.
- Training gives you options.
- Action creates trust in yourself.
- Control comes from knowing you can act.

**Content role:** Mid-funnel content (MOFU). Bridges from awareness
to action. Positions training as the answer to the problem exposed
in the Prepared stage. Shows the prospect what capability looks like.

**Emotional register:** Permission-giving. Activating.

---

### A — AWARE

**Identity state:** I see what others miss. I read environments.

**Core message:** Awareness is not paranoia. It is trained attention.

**Slogans:**
- Confidence starts with awareness, not bravado.
- Awareness is the first step toward control.
- Calm does not appear — it is built.
- The goal is not to live in condition red. It is to never be
  surprised by condition red.

**Content role:** Education content across all funnel stages. Awareness
is the foundational skill that connects every ETKM program — Krav Maga,
Fight Back ETX, ACT, EFC. This pillar generates the most shareable
social content because it applies to everyone, not just fighters.

**Emotional register:** Intelligent. Observant. Quiet confidence.

---

### C — CAPABLE

**Identity state:** I can act effectively under pressure.

**Core message:** Capability is earned through training, not wished into existence.

**Slogans:**
- Capability is earned, not imagined.
- Capability beats fear every time.
- Clarity under pressure is trained.
- You do not guess — you act.

**Content role:** Decision-stage content (BOFU). This is where training
outcomes become tangible. Student stories, skill demonstrations, and
proof of transformation live here. The prospect sees themselves as
someone who could become this.

**Emotional register:** Earned confidence. Quiet certainty.

---

### E — ENGAGED

**Identity state:** I am responsible for more than myself. I am a protector.

**Core message:** Prepared people become anchors for others.

**Slogans:**
- Prepared people become anchors for others.
- Readiness is a responsibility, not a luxury.
- You protect what matters because you prepared to.
- The people around you are safer because of the work you did.

**Content role:** Retention and identity content. Speaks to current
students and advanced practitioners. Reinforces the protector identity
that keeps students training long-term. Also serves as aspirational
content for prospects who see this as the person they want to become.

**Emotional register:** Weight. Responsibility. Purpose.

## How PEACE Maps to the Funnel

| Pillar | Funnel Stage | Content Type |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Prepared | TOFU — Awareness | Blog posts, social quotes, awareness content |
| Empowered | MOFU — Consideration | Training previews, program explainers, bridge content |
| Aware | All stages | Educational content, shareable social, situational awareness |
| Capable | BOFU — Decision | Student stories, testimonials, skill proof, trial CTAs |
| Engaged | Retention | Student emails, community content, protector identity |

## How PEACE Maps to the 52-Week Social Calendar

The 52-week PEACE slogan rotation (WF-004) organizes content into
four 13-week phases. Each phase blends multiple PEACE pillars but
emphasizes a progression:

| Weeks | Phase Theme | Primary PEACE Pillars |
|-------|------------|----------------------|
| 1-13 | Awareness to Reality to Readiness | Prepared + Aware |
| 14-26 | Agency to Action to Capability | Empowered + Capable |
| 27-39 | Identity to Empowerment to Presence | Empowered + Engaged |
| 40-52 | Leadership to Responsibility to Protector Mindset | Engaged + Capable |

## How PEACE Is Used in Email Campaigns

In the WF-003 CBLTAC campaign, PEACE slogans were integrated as
attention anchors — short branded phrases that open or punctuate
an email section. The pattern:

1. PEACE slogan as a section opener (bold, standalone line)
2. Content paragraph expanding on the slogan's theme
3. Bridge to specific event/training/CTA

This pattern is reusable across any ETKM email campaign. Match the
slogan to the email's emotional stage — Prepared slogans for early
sequence emails, Capable/Engaged slogans for decision-stage emails.

## PEACE and StoryBrand Alignment

| StoryBrand Element | PEACE Mapping |
|-------------------|---------------|
| Hero (the prospect) | Starts at Prepared — aware of the gap |
| Problem (external, internal, philosophical) | Prepared exposes it, Empowered names it |
| Guide (ETKM/Nathan) | Empowered and Aware — provides the path |
| Plan (3-step, training path) | Aware and Capable — the training itself |
| Call to Action | Capable — the decision moment |
| Success (transformation) | Engaged — the protector identity |
| Failure (what's at stake) | Contrast against Prepared — what happens without training |

## Rules for Using PEACE in Content

- Never spell out the acronym in prospect-facing content unless
  the context is explicitly educational. Let the slogans do the work.
- Each PEACE slogan should feel like it could stand alone on a poster.
  If it needs explanation to land, it is not ready for deployment.
- Do not stack multiple PEACE slogans in the same section. One per
  section maximum.
- Match the pillar to the audience's emotional state, not just the
  content topic. A post about awareness aimed at brand-new prospects
  uses Prepared language. The same topic aimed at Level 3 students
  uses Capable or Engaged language.
- PEACE is the framework. The slogans are the output. Never reference
  "the PEACE framework" in social posts or prospect emails — that is
  internal language.
