## The Funnel Architecture — Where Lead Gen PDFs Live

Lead gen PDFs are no longer the destination. They are the **on-ramp.**

```
Lead Gen PDF  →  Readiness Score Quiz  →  Personalized Deep PDF  →  Trial / Consult
```

**Stage 1 — Lead Gen PDF (this skill)**
Cold/warm audience. Persona-targeted. Delivers a genuine quick win. Builds enough
trust and curiosity that the reader wants to know their score. Every PDF ends with
two CTAs pointing to the same funnel.

**Stage 2 — Personal Safety Readiness Assessment (quiz)**
The prospect takes the scored assessment. Opt-in captured. Arc classified. Gaps
identified. Score and gap summary delivered immediately. This is the primary destination
every Lead Gen PDF drives toward.

**Stage 3 — Personalized Deep PDF**
Delivered post-quiz. Targeted to the prospect's specific gap profile. Book-intelligence
derived. This is what earns the trial booking conversation.

**Stage 4 — Trial Lesson / Consult**
Nathan walks in already knowing the score, arc, and gaps. Sales becomes coaching.

## The Lead Gen Philosophy

A lead magnet is a **value trade**, not a trick.

The prospect gives ETKM their contact information. ETKM gives them something genuinely
useful in return. The asset must be so immediately valuable that they feel good about the
trade — before they've ever walked through the door.

**The Lead Gen PDF now does four things:**
1. Delivers real, usable value (builds trust and credibility)
2. Gently positions ETKM as the guide with the answer
3. Seeds curiosity about the prospect's personal readiness — not just general knowledge
4. Drives toward the quiz as the natural, obvious next step

**What a lead magnet is NOT:**
- A brochure about ETKM
- A price list
- A hard sell in disguise
- A watered-down teaser that withholds the real value

## ETKM Primary Lead Gen Format: PDF Guides

PDFs are ETKM's primary digital lead gen format. They are chosen because:
- Low friction to create and distribute
- High perceived value (tangible, downloadable, saveable)
- Easy to tailor to specific personas
- Work across all digital channels (ads, email, social, website)
- Position ETKM as an authority and educator, not just a gym

## PDF Lead Magnet — Master Template Structure

Every ETKM PDF lead magnet follows this structure. The subject matter changes per persona
and campaign. The architecture does not.

---

### SECTION 1 — The Cover

**Elements:**
- ETKM logo (top)
- Headline: Bold, specific, persona-targeted benefit statement
- Subheadline: One sentence that clarifies who this is for and what they'll walk away with
- Hero image or graphic (brand-aligned — see etkm-brand-kit)
- Optional: "A free guide from East Texas Krav Maga"

**Headline Formula:**
[Number] [Specific Outcome] for [Target Persona] — Even If [Common False Belief]

Examples (structure only — subject matter TBD):
- "5 Things Every [Persona] Should Know About [Topic] — Even If You've Never Trained Before"
- "The [Persona]'s Guide to [Specific Outcome] in [Timeframe or Context]"
- "[Number] Ways to [Specific Action] — Starting Today"

---

### SECTION 2 — The Hook Page (Page 1 Interior)

**Purpose:** Confirm the reader made the right choice downloading this. Make them feel seen.

**Elements:**
- 2-3 sentence empathy statement that names their specific fear or frustration
- 1 sentence that validates the fear as real and legitimate
- 1 sentence that reframes: "But here's what most people don't know..."
- 1 sentence positioning ETKM as the guide: "That's exactly why we put this together."
- Brief permission statement: What they're about to learn and why it matters

**Voice:** Warm, direct, calm. Not lecture. Not sales. Conversation.

---

### SECTION 3 — The Core Content (2-5 pages)

**Purpose:** Deliver the promised value AND seed curiosity about the reader's personal readiness.

**Structure per content item:**
- Bold header (the tip, principle, or step)
- 2-4 sentences of explanation
- 1 practical application sentence — something they can do TODAY, without joining ETKM
- 1 self-reflection seed: a question or statement that makes them wonder about their own level
- Optional: A brief "most people don't realize" insight that adds depth

**The Same-Day Action Rule (NEW):**
Every content item must include something the reader can act on the same day they read it —
no training required, no equipment, no prior knowledge. The quick win is non-negotiable.
If the item doesn't produce a same-day action, rewrite it until it does.

**The Self-Reflection Seed Rule (NEW):**
Every content item should leave the reader with a question about themselves, not just
information. Not "here's what threat awareness is" — but "here's what threat awareness is,
and most people don't know where they actually fall on that scale." This is the curiosity
engine that drives quiz completion. It is never a hard sell. It is an honest observation
that makes the reader want to find out their own answer.

**Content rules:**
- Minimum 3 items, maximum 7 items per PDF
- Each item must stand alone as genuinely useful — not dependent on joining ETKM
- Each item should subtly reinforce the protector identity narrative
- No jargon without explanation
- No technique instruction that requires video — this is conceptual, not physical training

**False Belief Dismantle — Target the Overconfidence Belief (UPDATED):**
Every PDF must organically address the overconfidence false belief relevant to this funnel:
"I'm already pretty aware / I'd know what to do / I'm not the kind of person this happens to."
This is the primary belief blocking quiz completion. Bust it inside the content naturally —
never as a section, never as a lecture. One honest, specific example of how this belief
costs people. Do not make it scary. Make it real.

**One Real Person Rule (NEW):**
Every PDF must include at least one 2-3 sentence real-world example or anonymous student
outcome woven into the content — inside a principle, not in a testimonial box. This is not
a success story. It is proof of concept. Example: "One of our students — a nurse who works
late shifts — told us she'd always assumed she was alert on her walk to the car. After
learning this principle, she realized she'd been operating on autopilot for years."

---

### SECTION 4 — The Bridge (Second-to-Last Page)

**Purpose:** Move the reader from "I learned something" to "I want to know where I stand."

**The bridge no longer sells ETKM directly. It sells the quiz.**

**New Bridge Formula:**
"Now you know [what the PDF taught]. That's the knowledge side. The harder question is —
how would you actually perform if it mattered? Most people find out they're stronger in
some areas than they realized, and weaker in others they never thought about.
That's exactly why we built the Personal Safety Readiness Assessment.
It takes about 3 minutes. It tells you where you stand — specifically.
And it gives us what we need to point you toward the right next step for you."

**Tone:** Honest. Curious. Low pressure. This is a service, not a pitch.
Never: "Now that you've read this, it's time to sign up."
Always: "Here's the natural next question — and here's how to answer it."

---

### SECTION 5 — The CTA Page (Back Cover)

**Purpose:** Two clear paths. One page. Reader chooses based on where they are.

**Layout:**
- ETKM logo (top)
- CTA section headline: "Here's Your Next Step"
- Brief 1-2 sentence connector: "You've got the knowledge. Now find out how it applies to you."

**PRIMARY CTA — The Quiz (always first, always larger):**
Headline: "Find Out Where You Actually Stand"
Body: "Take the free Personal Safety Readiness Assessment. 3 minutes. Honest results.
We'll use your score to point you toward the right resources — no pressure, no sales pitch."
CTA text: "Take the Free Assessment"
URL: [Assessment landing page URL — TBD]
Optional: QR code linking to assessment landing page

**SECONDARY CTA — The Trial (always below, clearly secondary):**
Headline: "Rather Just Come In?"
Body: "That works too. Book a free trial lesson and we'll get started."
CTA text: "Book My Free Trial Lesson"
URL: etxkravmaga.com/trial

**What Happens Next micro-narrative (NEW):**
Below both CTAs, add 2 sentences that pre-sell the nurture sequence so email #1 doesn't
feel cold. Example: "Either way, you'll hear from us within a few minutes. No pressure —
just the next step, when you're ready."

## PDF Lead Magnet — Design Standards

> For full visual design specs, load etkm-brand-kit skill.

| Element | Standard |
|---|---|
| Length | 6-10 pages (including cover and CTA page) |
| Format | 8.5x11 / letter size PDF |
| Font | Per brand kit |
| Color palette | Per brand kit (black, white, red only) |
| Tone | Warm, direct, practical — matches brand voice |
| Logo placement | Cover + CTA page minimum |
| Images | Real people when possible, brand-aligned photography |
| No-go elements | Stock photos of people screaming, graphic violence imagery |

**Visual Density Rules (NEW):**
- Maximum 80 words per content item (header + body + action + seed combined)
- Minimum 30% white space per page — if the page looks full, it is too full
- Every content page must include at least one callout box, pull quote, or visual break
- No paragraph longer than 4 lines
- Bold is used for headers and key phrases only — not decoration

**Mobile Render Rule (NEW):**
Every PDF must be tested at mobile phone screen width before delivery.
Text must be readable without pinching or zooming. If it fails mobile render, it fails QC.
This gate is part of etkm-deliverable-qc.

## Opt-In Page Template (Landing Page Copy Structure)

When the PDF is delivered via a landing page, the page copy follows this structure:

**Headline:** Mirror the PDF cover headline
**Subheadline:** Clarify who it's for and what they get
**3-bullet value list:** Three things they'll learn or gain
**Lead capture form:** First name + email (minimum) — phone optional
**Privacy micro-copy:** "We respect your privacy. No spam. Unsubscribe anytime."
**Submit button copy:** NOT "Submit" — use action language:
  - "Send Me the Guide"
  - "Get My Free [Guide Name]"
  - "Yes, I Want This"

## Post-Download Nurture Handoff

When a prospect downloads a PDF, they enter the nurture sequence immediately.
The first follow-up email/text should arrive within 5 minutes of opt-in.

**Updated nurture sequence skeleton:**

| Touch | Timing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Email 1 | Immediate | Deliver the PDF + warm welcome |
| Email 2 | Day 2 | One additional quick-win tip — seeds quiz curiosity |
| Email 3 | Day 4 | Real student outcome story — busts overconfidence belief |
| Email 4 | Day 6 | Soft push to quiz: "We built something for exactly this" |
| Email 5 | Day 10 | Secondary CTA — trial lesson if quiz hasn't been taken |
| Email 6+ | Ongoing | Weekly value content — maintain relationship until ready |

> Full email copy lives in the nurture sequence skill.

## Quality Check — Every PDF Lead Magnet

Before finalizing any PDF, confirm:

- [ ] Would the prospect find this valuable even if they never joined ETKM?
- [ ] Does it speak directly and specifically to one persona's pain?
- [ ] Does it deliver the promised value — no bait-and-switch?
- [ ] Does every content item include a same-day action the reader can take today?
- [ ] Does every content item plant a self-reflection seed about their personal readiness?
- [ ] Does it address the overconfidence false belief naturally (not as a lecture)?
- [ ] Is there at least one real-world anonymous example woven into the content?
- [ ] Is ETKM positioned as guide — not hero, not salesperson?
- [ ] Does the Bridge sell the quiz — not a class, not a membership?
- [ ] Is the primary CTA the Readiness Assessment? Is the trial clearly secondary?
- [ ] Does the CTA page include the "what happens next" micro-narrative?
- [ ] Does it match brand voice (warm, direct, practical)?
- [ ] Is it 6-10 pages?
- [ ] Does every page pass visual density rules (80 words max, 30% white space)?
- [ ] Has the PDF been tested at mobile screen width and passed?
